# The Marketing Flywheel

For decades, marketers drew a funnel: pour leads in the top, push them through stages, count closed deals at the bottom. In 2018, HubSpot CEO Brian Halligan stood on stage at INBOUND and declared the funnel dead. He replaced it with a flywheel — and by 2025, that flywheel itself had evolved into a continuous loop. If you still plan campaigns as a funnel, you are designing for a buyer who no longer exists.

## What It Actually Is

A flywheel is a circular growth model where customers sit at the center and three forces — Attract, Engage, Delight — spin around them. The faster the wheel spins, the more momentum you build through word-of-mouth, referrals, renewals, and expansion. Friction (a bad onboarding, a slow support response, a confusing pricing page) slows the wheel. Force (a great review, a referral incentive, a helpful piece of content) speeds it up.

Concrete example: when a Notion user posts a template to Twitter and 200 people sign up because of it, the customer just became a marketing channel. That is the flywheel in motion — the output of the wheel feeds the input.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The economics favor the wheel over the funnel. McKinsey research repeatedly cited in the flywheel literature shows that a 5% increase in customer retention can lift profits by 25% to 95% ([Number Analytics](https://www.numberanalytics.com/blog/flywheel-fast-growth)). ChartMogul's 2024–2025 SaaS benchmarks put median Net Revenue Retention at 101–106%, with top-quartile B2B companies exceeding 120% — and McKinsey's 2024 analysis found those top-quartile retention companies trade at roughly 24x enterprise-value-to-revenue, nearly 5x the bottom quartile ([Influencers Time](https://www.influencers-time.com/transitioning-to-an-integrated-revenue-flywheel-model-in-2025/)).

The funnel ignores all of that. It treats the customer as an exit point. The flywheel treats the customer as the flywheel's biggest source of energy.

HubSpot doubled down at GROW Europe in November 2024, where executives argued that traditional funnels are now "broken" in an AI-mediated buyer journey ([Adrian Swinscoe](https://www.adrianswinscoe.com/2024/11/broken-funnels-flywheels-and-human-messaging-key-insights-from-hubspot-executives-at-grow-europe-2024/)). In 2025 HubSpot evolved the model again into the Loop Marketing Framework — Express, Tailor, Amplify, Evolve — designed for nonlinear, feedback-driven, AI-era marketing ([Page One Web Solutions](https://blog.pageonewebsolutions.com/loop-marketing-hubspots-new-framework)).

## How It Works / The Playbook

Map your wheel in four steps:

1. **Identify the three arcs.** Attract (content, SEO, paid, PR), Engage (sales, demos, free trial, onboarding), Delight (support, CS, community, advocacy).
2. **Audit your friction points.** Pull your CRM and find every place customers stall, churn, or complain. Each one is a brake on the wheel.
3. **Find your force multipliers.** Where are existing customers already generating new ones? Referrals, integrations, templates, UGC.
4. **Reallocate budget toward Delight.** Most companies spend 80% on Attract. Move 10–20 points toward retention, onboarding, and advocacy programs.

Run the math each quarter: track CAC payback, NRR, referral-sourced pipeline, and NPS together. If NRR is below 100%, the wheel is leaking and no amount of top-of-funnel spend will fix it.

<Callout type="example">
Amazon is the canonical flywheel: lower prices attract more customers, more customers attract more third-party sellers, more sellers expand selection, expanded selection drives more traffic, and the cycle compounds. Jeff Bezos sketched it on a napkin in 2001. By 2024, third-party sellers accounted for roughly 60% of Amazon's physical product sales — proof that the customer side of the wheel ended up powering the supply side too.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes

- **Drawing the flywheel but still measuring funnel KPIs.** If your weekly review is MQLs and SQLs only, you have a funnel with new branding.
- **Putting Sales at the center instead of the customer.** The wheel only spins if the customer's success is the axis. Otherwise you are just a circular funnel.
- **Ignoring friction inside Delight.** A 48-hour support SLA on a product with a 14-day trial kills the wheel before it starts. Measure time-to-value, not just time-to-close.

## Key Takeaways

- The funnel optimizes for one-time conversion; the flywheel optimizes for compounding retention and referrals.
- Customer retention up 5% can lift profits 25–95% — the math why the flywheel beats the funnel.
- HubSpot's 2025 Loop framework is the next evolution: continuous, AI-aware, feedback-driven. Read it, but ship the flywheel first.
