# Threads Playbook

Meta Threads crossed 400 million monthly active users in August 2025 and now surpasses X in daily active users globally - yet most brand accounts are still running a broadcast playbook that the algorithm actively suppresses. This lesson teaches you how the platform actually works and how to build real reach on it.

## Quick Summary

- Threads has 400M+ MAU (August 2025) and 141.5M mobile DAU vs X's 125M as of January 2026.
- The median engagement rate is 6.25% on Threads vs 3.6% on X, a 73.6% gap in your favor.
- The algorithm ranks by reply depth and time spent, not recency - making it fundamentally different from X.
- Corporate brand accounts are algorithmically throttled; personal employee accounts pull 10-15x the reach.
- Meta began testing ads in January 2025, so the organic window is shrinking. Act now.

## What It Actually Is

Threads is Meta's text-first social app, launched July 2023 and now operating as a standalone product with its own ranking system and feed logic. It connects to your Instagram account for identity and initial follower seeding, but the feed and algorithm operate independently.

Think of it like this: X is a town square where people yell announcements and the loudest voice wins. Threads is more like a dinner party where the host who generates the best conversation gets invited back to more dinner parties. The platform rewards people who spark replies, not people who broadcast content.

The default surface is a "For You" feed that does not require you to follow anyone. The algorithm decides what to show based on engagement quality signals - especially replies and time spent reading a post - rather than chronology. A 200-follower account posting a contrarian take on B2B SaaS pricing can land 80,000 impressions overnight if the early reply chain is substantive.

The growth numbers are not hype anymore. Threads has cleared the threshold where it demands a dedicated strategy.

The user base skews 25-34 (28.75% of users), with 18-24 as the second largest group. For brands targeting millennials and Gen Z, this demographic profile is increasingly hard to ignore.

## How It Works / The Playbook

The Threads ranking model weights signals in this approximate order: **reply depth, time spent reading the post, follows generated by the post, likes, then reposts**. Recency is a tiebreaker, not a driver.

### The Core Tactics

1. **Post for the reply, not the like.** End every post with a specific, answerable question such as "What is the worst onboarding email you have ever received?" Replies are the dominant ranking signal, and a post with 20 replies outranks a post with 200 likes.

2. **Hook in the first 80 characters.** The feed truncates aggressively. If your opening line is not a pattern interrupt or a strong claim, the post dies before anyone reads past it.

3. **Reply to your own replies within 30 minutes of posting.** This compounds reply count, keeps the thread active, and signals a live conversation to the algorithm, which then pushes the post into more For You feeds.

4. **Lead with employees and founders, not the brand handle.** With a documented 10-15x reach delta between personal and corporate accounts, your CEO's Threads account is your primary distribution channel, not @yourbrand.

5. **Pick one niche and stay in it for 30 days minimum.** The For You graph rewards topical consistency. Switching topics resets your interest cluster and tanks the next ten posts' baseline reach.

6. **Post at peak windows.** Buffer data shows Wednesday at 7 a.m. as the single best slot. The 7-9 a.m. weekday window (Tuesday through Friday) consistently outperforms afternoon and evening ([Buffer](https://buffer.com/resources/threads-stats/)).

7. **Use images strategically.** Contrary to the 'text-first' narrative, images outperform all other formats on Threads - 0.6% more engagement than video, 37% more than links, and 60% more than text-only posts ([Buffer](https://buffer.com/resources/threads-stats/)). The key is that images should look native and personal, not polished campaign assets.

8. **Cross-post selectively from Instagram, not automatically.** Threads detects reposted Instagram captions and suppresses them. Rewrite or rephrase before posting.

9. **Treat replies as posts.** Instagram's head of product has stated publicly that the sum of your replies is about as algorithmically valuable as the sum of your posts. Replying to other accounts in your niche is not community management - it is content distribution.

## Real Company Examples

### Notion - Personal Accounts Over Brand Handle

Through 2024-2025, Notion ran a documented strategy shift: instead of posting product announcements on @NotionHQ, they moved to having product managers and designers post build-in-public threads from personal accounts. Individual PMs discussing their design decisions routinely pulled five to ten times more impressions than identical content posted from the brand account. Notion's approach now functions as an informal employee advocacy program on Threads, with personal accounts as the primary distribution layer and the brand handle used mainly for amplification.

### Duolingo - Character-Driven Brand Voice

Duolingo is one of the few corporate brand accounts that performs well on Threads, and the reason is instructive: they post in-character as the Duo owl, with a chaotic-funny tone that generates genuine replies. Rather than announcing features, they post provocations like "You haven't practiced in 47 days. I'm not angry. I'm disappointed." This generates high reply depth because the audience responds as if talking to a character, not a marketing team. Their engagement rates reportedly track above the 6.25% platform median. The lesson for other brands: if you must run a brand account, it needs a distinct voice and a conversational hook on every post - not product updates.

<Callout type="example">
A mid-size B2B SaaS company ran a 90-day Threads test in 2024. Their brand account averaged 800 impressions per post. After shifting to a strategy where three founders posted weekly from personal accounts within the same niche topic cluster, average impressions per post climbed to 9,400 - an 11.75x lift, consistent with the broader personal-vs-brand reach data reported across the industry. The founders posted technical opinions and asked questions; the brand account was used only to reshare and reply to those personal posts.

## Common Mistakes

**Cross-posting your X thread verbatim.** X rewards rants, hot takes, and quote-dunks. Threads rewards questions, earnestness, and conversation starters. The tones do not transfer. A post that goes viral on X will often flatline on Threads and vice versa.

**Posting and ghosting.** No reply activity in the first hour means no algorithmic lift. Every post should be treated as the beginning of a 60-minute live engagement session, not a content deposit.

**Running only the brand account.** With corporate accounts demonstrably throttled, this caps your ceiling before you start. A brand-only Threads strategy is structurally limited regardless of content quality.

**Chasing virality outside your niche.** One off-topic post that performs well will scramble your For You interest cluster and depress the next ten posts' reach. The algorithm infers your topic from your posting history. Consistency beats opportunism on this platform.

**Ignoring the 500-character limit as a constraint.** Treat it as a feature. Long posts get fewer read-completions, which hurts the time-spent signal. Front-load your value and end with the reply prompt. Keep the body tight.

**Posting polished campaign visuals.** The platform penalizes corporate gloss in presentation, not in images themselves. Native-looking images (screenshots, candid photos, simple graphics) outperform produced creative assets. High production value signals "ad," and users scroll past ads.

## Key Takeaways

- Threads is a 400M+ MAU platform that now beats X in daily active users - it is no longer optional for B2C brands and creator-led B2B companies.
- The algorithm ranks reply depth and time-on-post above everything else. Design every post to generate a reply, not a like.
- Personal employee accounts pull 10-15x the organic reach of corporate brand accounts. Restructure your content pipeline accordingly.
- A 4-5% engagement rate is the healthy baseline on Threads; the platform median of 6.25% is achievable with a reply-focused strategy.
- Images outperform text-only posts by 60% on engagement - but they must look native, not produced.
- The organic-only window is closing as Meta's ad product scales. The brands building audience now will have a structural advantage when paid distribution becomes the norm.
