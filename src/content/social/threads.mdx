# Threads Playbook

Threads is not 'Twitter for Meta.' It is a conversational feed wired into Instagram's graph, ranked by an algorithm that punishes broadcast-style brand posts and rewards replies, niches, and individual voices. If you are a marketer treating it like an X clone, you are leaving most of the reach on the table.

## What It Actually Is

Threads is Meta's text-first social app, launched July 2023 and now run as a standalone product with its own ranking system. The default surface is a "For You" feed that does not require you to follow anyone — the algorithm decides what to show based on engagement signals (replies, time-on-post, follows triggered by the post) rather than recency. Example: a 200-follower account posting a contrarian take on B2B SaaS pricing can land 80,000 impressions overnight if early replies are substantive, because the For You feed treats reply depth as the strongest positive signal.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The growth story is real and the engagement gap is wider than most marketers realize.

- Threads passed 400 million monthly active users in August 2025, up from 275 million at the end of 2024 ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/threads-statistics/)).
- Daily active users hit 150 million by October 2025, and Similarweb data put Threads at 141.5M mobile DAU vs X's 125M by January 2026 ([Backlinko](https://backlinko.com/threads-users)).
- Median engagement rate on Threads is 6.25%, vs 3.6% on X — a 73.6% higher interaction rate ([WebFX Threads Benchmarks](https://www.webfx.com/blog/social-media/threads-marketing-benchmarks/)).
- Employees sharing branded content from personal Threads accounts are pulling 10–15x the organic reach of the official brand account, because the algorithm explicitly down-weights corporate voices ([WebFX](https://www.webfx.com/blog/social-media/threads-marketing-benchmarks/)).
- Meta began testing ads in the US and Japan in January 2025 — the organic-only window is closing.

## How It Works / The Playbook

The Threads ranking model weights, in rough order: reply depth, time spent reading the post, follows generated by the post, likes, then reposts. Recency is a tiebreaker, not a driver. Here is what to actually do:

1. **Post for the reply, not the like.** End posts with a specific, answerable prompt ("What is the worst onboarding email you have ever received?"). Replies are the dominant ranking signal.
2. **Hook in the first 80 characters.** The feed truncates aggressively; if your first line is not a pattern interrupt, the post dies.
3. **Reply to your own replies within 30 minutes.** This compounds reply count and signals an active conversation, which the algorithm boosts into more For You feeds.
4. **Lead with employees and founders, not the brand handle.** With 10–15x reach delta documented, your CEO's account is your distribution channel.
5. **Pick one niche and stay there for 30 days.** The For You graph rewards topical consistency — switching topics resets your interest cluster.
6. **Text first, image second.** Polished campaign visuals underperform plain-text takes. The format penalises corporate gloss.
7. **Cross-post selectively from Instagram, not automatically.** Threads detects reposted IG captions and suppresses them.

## Common Mistakes

- **Cross-posting your X thread verbatim.** X rewards rants and quote-dunks; Threads rewards questions and earnestness. The tones do not transfer.
- **Posting and ghosting.** No reply activity in the first hour = no algorithmic lift. Treat every post as the start of a 60-minute live session.
- **Running the brand account as your only presence.** With corporate accounts demonstrably throttled, this caps your ceiling before you start.
- **Chasing virality with hot takes outside your niche.** One off-topic banger will scramble your For You clustering and depress your next ten posts.
- **Ignoring the 500-character limit as a constraint.** It is a feature — long posts get fewer reads-to-completion, which hurts the time-spent signal.

## Key Takeaways

- Threads is now a 400M+ MAU platform with ~2x the engagement rate of X — it is no longer optional for B2C and creator-led B2B brands.
- The algorithm rewards replies and personal accounts; broadcast-style brand posting is structurally disadvantaged.
- Win by posting reply-bait questions in a tight niche, then actively replying for the first hour. Empower employees to post from personal accounts.
