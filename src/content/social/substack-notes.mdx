# Substack and Newsletter-Social

Every social platform is a rented apartment. Substack is the rare one where the algorithm's explicit goal is to hand you a portable email list you can take with you. If you write, teach, or sell anything that benefits from a recurring relationship, Notes is the cheapest distribution channel of 2025.

## What It Actually Is

Substack Notes is a Twitter-like short-form feed bolted onto Substack's newsletter product. The crucial difference: when someone restacks (Substack's retweet) or likes your Note, the platform pushes them toward subscribing to your newsletter, not toward more scrolling. The conversion unit is an email address, not a follow.

Concrete example: a writer posts a 200-word Note about a contrarian B2B insight. A reader restacks it. That restack shows up in 50 other people's feeds with a one-click subscribe button to the writer's Substack — no profile visit, no link-in-bio funnel.

## Why It Matters (with data)

Substack disclosed that the platform crossed [over 20 million active subscribers and more than 5 million paid subscriptions](https://writtenmarketing.com/p/substack-algorithm-2025) in 2024-2025, with Notes acting as the primary discovery surface. Substack's head of ML Mike Cohen and co-founder Hamish McKenzie have stated the recommendation system optimizes for subscriptions, not session time — a structural decision that makes the platform behave very differently from X or LinkedIn.

Creator results back this up. In a [public 2026 strategy review](https://escapethecubicle.substack.com/p/my-substack-year-in-review-100k-revenue), one creator documented hitting $100K in revenue and 15,000 subscribers in a year, attributing the majority of net-new subs to Notes. Another writer reported [60% of total growth coming from Notes alone](https://thrivewithcarrie.substack.com/p/substack-notes-strategy-2026), with 20-30 minutes of daily posting producing roughly 70% of new subscribers in their cohort.

A single high-performing "generosity Note" — a post recommending other writers in the niche — was documented driving 1,400 likes, 230 restacks, and 650 new subscribers from one piece of content. That conversion rate (subscribers per impression) is roughly an order of magnitude better than what's typical on X or Threads for the same author.

## How It Works / The Playbook

The Substack algorithm rewards three signals above all: restacks, replies from established accounts, and subscriber-conversion rate per impression. Optimize for those, not for like counts.

1. **Post 1-3 Notes per day, every day for 90 days.** Frequency is the entry ticket. Creators who post weekly stall; daily posters compound.
2. **Lead with one of four formats that consistently work in 2025:**
   - Contrarian take ("Everyone says X. Here is why X is wrong.")
   - Vulnerable confession ("I made $0 from my newsletter for 14 months. Here is what I did wrong.")
   - Generosity Note (publicly recommend 5-10 writers in your niche by @-handle)
   - Behind-the-scenes process (screenshot of your draft, your analytics, your reading list)
3. **Restack 5-10 Notes from larger accounts daily with a quote-comment.** This is the single fastest way to get on bigger writers' radar. Their restack of your restack is worth more than 10 of your own posts.
4. **Convert Notes to long-form.** Every Note that breaks 100 likes is a signal — turn it into a full newsletter issue within 7 days while the audience is warm.
5. **Turn on Recommendations.** Recommend 8-12 other Substacks. Most will reciprocate; this is the platform's built-in growth loop and currently the single highest-ROI button on the product.

## Common Mistakes

- **Treating Notes like Twitter.** Threading, ratio-baiting, and engagement-farming hooks underperform here because the algorithm doesn't reward dwell time — it rewards restacks from people whose audience overlaps with yours.
- **Linking out in every Note.** External links suppress reach. Save the link for your pinned welcome Note or the newsletter itself.
- **Posting only when you publish a newsletter.** The Substack creators who flatlined in the September 2024 algorithm shift were almost all weekly-only posters. Daily Note posters absorbed the change.
- **Ignoring Recommendations.** Creators who skip Recommendations leave roughly 30-50% of organic growth on the table, per multiple 2025 public case studies.
- **Chasing follower count.** Followers on Substack are nearly worthless — only email subscribers compound. Optimize the funnel for the subscribe click.

## Key Takeaways

- Substack is the only major social platform whose algorithm is explicitly tuned to hand you a portable email list.
- Daily Notes posting plus active Recommendations is currently the highest-ROI organic growth combination in creator marketing.
- The four formats that work in 2025 are contrarian takes, vulnerable confessions, generosity Notes, and behind-the-scenes process.
- Measure subscribers-per-Note, not likes. A 50-like Note that drives 30 subs beats a 500-like Note that drives 5.
