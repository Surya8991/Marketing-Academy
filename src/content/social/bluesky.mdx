# Bluesky and the Open Social Web

Bluesky is the first decentralized social network to cross 40 million users, and the AT Protocol underneath it changes the rules marketers have lived by for a decade. If you run brand or growth at a company that depends on social distribution, you need to understand what happens when the algorithm, the identity layer, and the moderation stack are no longer owned by one company.

## What It Actually Is

Bluesky is a microblogging app (think Twitter circa 2012) built on the AT Protocol — an open standard where your identity, your follow graph, and your posts live in a Personal Data Server you can move between providers. The closest analogy: email. Your address can move from Gmail to Fastmail without losing your contacts. On Bluesky, your handle and followers can move from bsky.social to a self-hosted server without anyone's permission.

The marketer-relevant consequence: there is no single algorithm. Users subscribe to "custom feeds" — third-party-built algorithms — and pick which one runs their timeline.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The platform grew nearly 60% in 2025, from 25.94M to 41.41M users, and users posted 1.41 billion times that year — 61% of all posts ever made on the network ([Bluesky 2025 Transparency Report](https://bsky.social/about/blog/01-29-2026-transparency-report-2025)). Daily active users sit around 4.1 million as of mid-2025 ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/bluesky-statistics/)).

Three numbers matter more than the headline growth:

- **50,000+ custom algorithmic feeds** are live, built by users and third parties via SkyFeed and similar tools ([Mindgruve](https://mindgruve.com/blog/strategy/bluesky-a-strategic-guide-for-brands-in-2025)).
- **62% of users are under 35**, with the largest cohorts being tech, finance, and creative professionals — a high-LTV audience that left X.
- **Daily anti-social behavior reports dropped ~79%** after the platform shipped reply toxicity detection, materially improving brand safety.

The US accounts for 50.13% of users, with Japan and Brazil as the next two growth markets.

## How It Works / The Playbook

Bluesky has no paid ads as of 2026. Distribution is earned. The mental model is closer to a podcast network than a paid social platform.

1. **Claim a verified domain handle.** Set your handle to `@yourbrand.com` via DNS — it is free, instant, and acts as built-in verification.
2. **Submit to relevant custom feeds.** Identify the 5–10 feeds your audience subscribes to (e.g., "Marketing Pros", "Science", regional feeds) and post content that matches their curation rules. Getting into a 50K-subscriber niche feed beats shouting into the For You algorithm.
3. **Build or sponsor a feed.** Brands with engineering can publish their own custom feed — a permanent, opt-in distribution channel users actively choose. There is no equivalent on Meta or TikTok.
4. **Use starter packs.** A starter pack is a one-tap follow list. Brands that ship a useful starter pack ("30 climate scientists worth following") get bulk follows in return.
5. **Optimize for replies, not reposts.** The default "Following" algorithm surfaces conversation. Posts that spark threads outperform link-drops 10:1 anecdotally — plan for staff to engage in replies for 30 minutes after posting.

The campaign worked because Bluesky users self-select into topical feeds — Blueland could reach gardeners, low-waste advocates, and climate journalists through three different custom feeds with one content set.

## Common Mistakes

- **Cross-posting from X verbatim.** Bluesky's culture punishes link-bait headlines and engagement-farming threads. Posts that hit 1M on X regularly die at 50 likes here.
- **Ignoring the protocol layer.** Treating Bluesky as just an app means you miss the real moat: building on AT Proto (custom feeds, labelers, moderation services) creates owned distribution no algorithm change can take away.
- **Buying followers or running giveaway loops.** No paid amplification exists, and follower-graph spam is heavily down-ranked by community-run labelers.
- **Skipping domain verification.** Unverified handles get reported as impersonation; DNS verification takes 10 minutes and prevents brand-protection headaches.

## Key Takeaways

- Bluesky is not a Twitter clone — it is a protocol with an app on top. Marketers who learn AT Proto get a structural advantage over those who do not.
- Distribution lives in custom feeds, not a single algorithm. Mapping feeds is the new keyword research.
- The audience is small (4M DAU) but high-value: under-35, professional, and actively choosing the platform over X. Treat it like a B2B channel, not a mass-reach one.
