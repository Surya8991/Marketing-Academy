# Bluesky and the Open Social Web

Bluesky crossed 40 million registered users in late 2025 after a 302% growth surge in just over a year, and it operates on fundamentally different rules than any platform you have managed before. There are no paid ads, no central algorithm, and no single company controlling who sees your content - which means organic skill is the only lever that works.

## Quick Summary

- Bluesky is built on the AT Protocol, an open standard that lets users own their identity, followers, and data independently of any one company.
- The platform reached 40.2 million users by November 2025, growing from 10 million in September 2024 ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/bluesky-statistics/)).
- There are zero paid advertising options as of mid-2026. Every impression is earned through content, community, or feed placement.
- Distribution runs through 50,000+ community-built custom feeds instead of a single algorithmic timeline.
- Early publishers report 2-10x higher engagement on Bluesky compared to platforms where they have far larger audiences (per Bluesky COO Rose Wang).

## What It Actually Is

Bluesky is a microblogging platform - short posts, replies, image attachments, links - sitting on top of the AT Protocol, an open technical standard for decentralized social networks. Think of it like email: your Gmail address moves to Fastmail without losing your contacts or history. On Bluesky, your handle, followers, and posts live in a Personal Data Server you control. You can move them between hosting providers without anyone's permission.

The practical consequence for marketers: there is no single company holding your audience hostage. Your followers belong to you, not to the platform. The tradeoff is that there is also no paid amplification infrastructure to rent your way into feeds.

## Why It Matters (with data)

Bluesky's growth in 2025 was driven by three external events: Brazil banned X in late 2024 (adding 2.6 million users in days), the US presidential election in November 2024 drove a wave from 15 million to 22.5 million users, and general X fatigue continued pushing professionals off the platform ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/bluesky-statistics/)).

The numbers that matter for your planning:

- **40.2 million registered users** as of November 2025, on track to exceed 42 million by early 2026.
- **1.5 to 3 million daily active users**, representing an 8-9% DAU-to-registered ratio - comparable to early Twitter, not mature Facebook.
- **Average session: 10 minutes 35 seconds.** Users come to participate, not scroll. This is higher than the industry average for passive-consumption apps.
- **50,000+ custom algorithmic feeds** are live and actively used, built by independent developers via tools like SkyFeed ([Mindgruve](https://mindgruve.com/blog/strategy/bluesky-a-strategic-guide-for-brands-in-2025)).
- **Anti-social behavior reports dropped 79%** after Bluesky shipped reply toxicity detection in 2025, making it the safest major social platform for brand adjacency.
- **US users: 50.13%** of traffic. Japan (6.03%) and Brazil (4.06%) are the next largest markets.
- **Demographics: 32% under 24, 26% aged 25-34.** The 18-34 cohort is the dominant group, with a strong skew toward tech, journalism, academia, and creative industries.

The audience is small by Meta or TikTok standards, but it is unusually high-intent. Users opted out of algorithmically manipulated feeds and chose a platform with no ads. That self-selection creates a very different content consumption posture.

## How It Works / The Playbook

Bluesky requires a different operational model than any paid-or-organic platform you have run before. The core shift: you are mapping a feed ecosystem, not optimizing for one algorithm.

### Step 1: Establish Your Identity Layer

Set your handle to `@yourbrand.com` via DNS TXT record. This is free, takes under 10 minutes, and functions as instant verification. Unverified handles starting with `@username.bsky.social` get flagged as potential impersonation by community labelers.

### Step 2: Map the Custom Feed Landscape

Before posting a single piece of content, identify which custom feeds your target audience subscribes to. Tools like SkyFeed and Blueskyfeedcreator let you browse feeds by topic and subscriber count. A 50,000-subscriber niche feed in your industry is worth more than posting into the general chronological timeline.

Key feed categories to investigate:
- Industry-specific feeds (e.g., "Marketing Pros", "SaaS Founders", "Climate Tech")
- Topic-keyword feeds that auto-pull posts containing specific terms
- Geographic feeds for regional markets
- Community-curated feeds run by respected accounts in your niche

### Step 3: Post Content That Earns Feed Inclusion

Each custom feed has curation rules: some use keyword matching, some rely on human curation, some use engagement signals. Post content that matches the explicit topic focus of your target feeds. Promotional content rarely survives curation. Educational, opinionated, or data-driven posts do.

Post format performance on Bluesky (based on creator reporting):
- **Thread posts** with genuine analysis: highest reply volume
- **Link posts**: not throttled unlike X, strong click-through for publishers
- **Image posts**: perform well in visual-niche feeds
- **Promotional announcements**: significantly underperform across all feed types

### Step 4: Build or Sponsor a Custom Feed

Brands with developer resources can publish a custom feed as a permanent distribution channel. A feed is an opt-in subscription your audience actively chooses, not an algorithm you are competing against. There is no equivalent on Meta, TikTok, or LinkedIn. A brand-published feed covering a topic your audience cares about (not just your products) can accumulate subscribers and generate sustained reach independently of your follower count.

### Step 5: Ship Starter Packs Strategically

A starter pack is a curated one-tap follow list. When a new user joins Bluesky, they are prompted to select starter packs to bootstrap their feed. A well-named pack ("20 supply chain experts worth following", "Essential climate journalists") gets discovered in onboarding flows. Brands that appear in or sponsor useful starter packs report bulk follower gains with no paid spend.

### Step 6: Invest 30 Minutes of Reply Time Per Post

The default Following feed is reverse-chronological and surfaces conversation threads. A post with 50 replies and 10 likes signals more community health than a post with 200 likes and 2 replies. Plan for a team member to engage in replies for 30 minutes after every major post. This is not optional on Bluesky - it is the primary amplification mechanism.

<Callout type="info">
On Bluesky, replies are the distribution engine. A reply from a respected account in a niche can introduce your content to their entire follower graph through thread surfacing. Prioritize replying to relevant conversations before you post your own content - many brands have built audiences faster through replies than through original posts.
</Callout>

## Real Company Examples

### Blueland: Micro-Influencer Playbook with 13x ROI

Eco-cleaning brand Blueland ran a campaign in early 2025 partnering with 211 micro-influencers (accounts with 1,000-10,000 followers) to seed its Amazon product line. The total investment was under $10,000 and returned 13x ROI. The strategy worked because Bluesky's custom feeds let the brand concentrate distribution across three specific communities simultaneously: zero-waste advocates, gardening enthusiasts, and climate journalists - all subscribed to different feeds but reachable with a single content strategy. The topical self-selection of Bluesky's user base made micro-influencer targeting far more precise than equivalent spend on Instagram ([Sprinklr](https://www.sprinklr.com/blog/bluesky-marketing/)).

### Hank Green: Link Performance Without Throttling

Creator and entrepreneur Hank Green ran a direct comparison experiment in 2025, posting identical content containing outbound links on Bluesky and on X. Link posts on Bluesky received thousands of likes; the same posts on X received "maybe a few dozen." Bluesky does not algorithmically penalize content that links outside the platform. For publishers, newsletters, and content-driven brands, this is a structural advantage that changes the economics of traffic acquisition ([Sprinklr](https://www.sprinklr.com/blog/bluesky-marketing/)).

## Common Mistakes

**Cross-posting from X without adaptation.** Bluesky's culture explicitly rejects the content formats that perform on X: dunks, engagement bait, hot takes designed for quote-tweet amplification, and promotional announcements. Posts optimized for X regularly fail on Bluesky. Rewrite for substance and conversation, not for virality.

**Treating the platform as an app instead of a protocol.** The strategic moat is not your follower count - it is ownership of your data and portability of your audience. Brands that only focus on the Bluesky app and ignore AT Protocol capabilities (building feeds, running labelers, hosting their own PDS) are leaving the most defensible advantages unused.

**Skipping domain verification.** Operating as `@brandname.bsky.social` instead of `@brandname.com` creates impersonation risk. Community-run labelers on Bluesky flag unverified brand-name handles. DNS verification takes 10 minutes and costs nothing.

**Optimizing for post frequency over reply quality.** High-volume posting without community engagement signals spam behavior to the feed curation algorithms. One post that generates 20 replies outperforms 10 posts that generate zero replies.

**Measuring success with vanity metrics.** Follower counts on Bluesky are less meaningful than feed inclusion rates and reply engagement. A brand with 2,000 followers included in a 40,000-subscriber niche feed is reaching more relevant people than a brand with 20,000 followers posting into the chronological void.

## Key Takeaways

- Bluesky is a protocol with an app on top. Brands that learn AT Protocol build distribution infrastructure that no platform policy change can erase.
- Custom feeds replace the single algorithm. Mapping feed ecosystems is the new keyword research - do it before you write a single post.
- The platform has zero paid ads. Every impression is earned through content quality, community engagement, or feed placement strategy.
- Session length (10 min 35 sec) and engagement rates (2-10x vs. comparable platforms) confirm Bluesky users are high-intent and participatory, not passive scrollers.
- Domain-verified handles are free and take 10 minutes. There is no valid reason to run an unverified brand account.
- Replies are the primary amplification mechanism. Budget 30 minutes of reply time per post and treat incoming replies as the main distribution event, not an afterthought.
