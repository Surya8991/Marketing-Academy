# Organic vs. Paid Social

If you posted to a Facebook Page in 2012, roughly 16% of your followers saw it. In 2025, that number is closer to 1.2%. This lesson is for marketers and founders trying to figure out where to put their time and money on social — and why "just post more" stopped working years ago.

## What It Actually Is

**Organic social** is anything you post for free: a tweet, a Reel, a LinkedIn carousel, a TikTok. The platform decides who sees it based on its algorithm and your follower count.

**Paid social** is anything you pay the platform to distribute: a boosted post, a Meta Ads campaign, a TikTok Spark Ad, a LinkedIn Sponsored Post. You pay per impression, click, or conversion, and you choose the audience.

Example: a SaaS founder records a 60-second product demo. Posting it from the company LinkedIn page is organic. Spending $500 to push it to "Marketing Managers at companies with 50-200 employees" is paid.

## Why It Matters (with data)

Organic reach has been in freefall for over a decade, and 2024-2025 made it worse:

- Facebook Page organic reach sits at roughly **1.20% to 1.64%** in 2024-2025, down from 16% in 2012, per [Socialinsider](https://www.socialinsider.io/blog/social-media-reach/) and [Campaign Pros](https://campaignpros.io/learning-center/facebook-organic-reach-decline).
- Instagram dropped to a **3.50% average reach rate** in 2025, a 12% year-over-year decline ([Socialinsider](https://www.socialinsider.io/blog/social-media-reach/)).
- TikTok, long the organic-friendly outlier, saw a **50% drop in organic engagement in 2024** ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/organic-vs-paid-social-media/)).

Meanwhile, paid is getting more expensive. Meta's average price per ad rose **10% in 2024**, and paid social spend grew **13% year-over-year** ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/organic-vs-paid-social-media/)). The platforms have engineered a closed loop: organic reach falls, brands buy ads to compensate, demand pushes ad prices up.

The implication is blunt: if you have a "social media strategy" that depends on organic reach from a brand page, you are budgeting against a graph that has only gone down for 13 years.

## How It Works / The Playbook

A working 2025 social strategy uses both, but assigns them different jobs.

1. **Use organic for proof, not distribution.** Treat your feed as a portfolio that prospects check after they hear about you elsewhere. Post 2-4 times per week, not 14. Quality and consistency beat volume.
2. **Use paid for cold reach.** If you need a new audience to see something this week, pay for it. Boosting a post that already performed organically is the highest-ROI starting move — you let the algorithm pre-validate the creative for free.
3. **Build creator partnerships instead of follower counts.** A single post from a creator with 30k engaged followers in your niche will out-reach your own page by 10x. This is why TikTok Spark Ads and Instagram Partnership Ads exist.
4. **Pick one platform to win.** Beginners spread across 5 platforms and lose on all of them. Pick the one where your buyer actually scrolls.
5. **Measure paid on CAC, organic on engaged followers and inbound DMs.** They are not the same KPI. Do not compare them on impressions.

<Callout type="example">
Duolingo built a 7-million-follower TikTok account organically between 2021-2023 with its unhinged owl mascot — one of the last great organic-only success stories. By 2024, even Duolingo had shifted to a hybrid model, running paid TikTok campaigns to push specific course launches, because their own data showed organic posts now reached a small fraction of their followers. The takeaway: even the textbook winners now pay for distribution ([Sprinklr](https://www.sprinklr.com/blog/organic-vs-paid-social-media/)).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes

- **Boosting every post.** Boosting is the worst-targeted, most-expensive paid product Meta sells. Use Ads Manager with proper targeting instead.
- **Judging organic by reach.** Reach is dead as an organic metric. Measure saves, shares, profile visits, and DMs — the signals that correlate with pipeline.
- **Running paid without a landing page.** Sending paid traffic to your Instagram profile wastes 80% of the spend. Send it to a page you control.
- **Cross-posting identical content to 5 platforms.** A square Instagram graphic dies on TikTok. Each platform needs native formatting.
- **Treating influencer fees as "organic."** If you paid for the post, it is paid social — track it as such.

## Key Takeaways

- Organic reach on Facebook and Instagram has collapsed to 1-4%. Plan accordingly.
- Organic builds trust and proof; paid buys reach. Stop expecting one to do the other's job.
- The highest-leverage paid tactic is amplifying creative that already won organically.
- One platform done well beats five done poorly — especially for solo founders and small teams.
