# How Social Algorithms Work

Your follower count is no longer a distribution channel. Every major platform now runs an interest-graph model where each post competes for reach from scratch, scored against predicted user behavior, not who follows you.

## Quick Summary

- All major platforms shifted from follow-graph to interest-graph ranking. Your followers are a weak input.
- AI now drives 80%+ of content recommendations across platforms, per 2025 industry data.
- The dominant signal differs by platform: completion rate on TikTok, sends on Instagram Reels, dwell time on LinkedIn, AVD plus CTR on YouTube.
- Saves and shares now outweigh likes on every major platform. Likes are the cheapest, lowest-signal action you can earn.
- The first 60 minutes after posting is a diagnostic window. Early engagement velocity determines whether the algorithm promotes a post to a second-tier audience.

## What It Actually Is

A social algorithm is a real-time ranking system that scores every candidate post for every user session and sorts the feed by predicted value. Think of it as an auction that runs invisibly every time someone opens an app. Instead of money, the currency is predicted probability of meaningful action: will this user watch to the end, save it, share it, or comment?

The "value score" for a post is roughly: (probability of watch completion x weight) + (probability of save x weight) + (probability of share x weight) + (probability of comment x weight), then multiplied by a quality and safety modifier. Platforms tune the weights differently, which is why the same content performs entirely differently across apps.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The numbers show how dramatically the shift to interest-graph ranking has changed reach. Instagram organic reach now sits at 5 to 7.6% per post, down 12% from 2024 to 2025, according to [AutoFaceless's 2025-2026 algorithm statistics report](https://autofaceless.ai/blog/social-media-algorithm-statistics-2026). LinkedIn saw a 34% organic reach decline from 2024 to 2025 as the platform tightened quality filters. Facebook business pages reach just 2.6 to 5.9% of followers organically.

TikTok is the outlier. Its engagement rate stands at 3.70%, a 49% year-over-year increase, because its algorithm is purpose-built for discovery rather than social graphs. TikTok's watch time and completion rate account for 40 to 50% of the algorithm's total weight, per [Fanpage Karma's 2025 analysis](https://www.fanpagekarma.com/insights/the-2025-tiktok-algorithm-what-you-need-to-know/). Aim for 70%+ video completion to trigger wider distribution; below 30% and the video gets throttled.

On LinkedIn, dwell time is now one of the top three ranking factors according to Social Media Examiner's October 2025 analysis. Comments carry roughly 15x the weight of likes, per [Meet-Lea's algorithm breakdown](https://meet-lea.com/en/blog/linkedin-algorithm-explained). LinkedIn's 360Brew, a 150-billion-parameter foundation model deployed in late 2024, replaced thousands of legacy recommendation models and placed even heavier emphasis on time-spent signals. An 800-word post that someone reads for two minutes can beat a meme that earns 200 quick likes and zero dwell time.

Saves are now the strongest ranking signal on Instagram and YouTube, replacing likes as the clearest vote of content value. Meta's RankNet-7 AI reduced content abandonment by 27.4% by deprioritizing content that users skip quickly, making early drop-off a serious penalty.

## How It Works / The Playbook

Every major feed algorithm runs the same four-stage pipeline under the hood.

What you can control sits almost entirely in stage 3, the heavy ranking stage. Each platform weights signals differently, so optimize for the platform's dominant signal rather than trying to maximize all signals at once.

### TikTok

- **Completion rate is everything.** Hook in the first 1 to 2 seconds. Design loops, cliffhangers, or reveals that pull viewers past the halfway mark.
- Target 70%+ completion rate. Videos crossing that threshold get pushed to a dramatically larger audience pool.
- Saves signal "I want to come back to this." Add a tip, a checklist, or a screenshot-worthy stat to every video.
- Shares and saves now outweigh likes after TikTok's 2025 algorithm update. Optimize for depth over breadth.

### Instagram Reels

- **Sends-per-reach is the metric Adam Mosseri has cited most often.** Make content someone wants to DM to a specific friend or group.
- Saves are the second most powerful signal. Useful information, tutorials, and reference content earn saves; entertainment earns views.
- Captions now function as indexing tools. Instagram's 2025 update made keyword-based discovery more prominent, so write captions that describe the content precisely.
- Reels account for 20%+ of time spent on Instagram. Investing in short video is not optional.

### LinkedIn

- **Dwell time is the dominant signal.** A post someone reads for 45 seconds beats a post that gets 100 quick likes.
- Write long-form posts with clear line breaks. Native carousels (PDF documents uploaded directly) consistently outperform external links because they keep users on the platform.
- Reply to every comment in the first 60 minutes. LinkedIn uses the first hour as a diagnostic window to decide whether to amplify a post network-wide.
- Quality of comments matters, not just quantity. Twelve substantive replies drive more ranking signal than 50 generic reactions, per 2025 analysis.

### YouTube

- Click-through rate on the thumbnail combined with average view duration (AVD) are the two primary signals.
- The first 30 seconds determine whether viewers stay. Deliver on the thumbnail's promise immediately.
- Shorts follow TikTok-style completion rate logic. Long-form videos are ranked on AVD as a percentage of total length, not raw minutes watched.

### X (formerly Twitter)

- Replies and bookmarks now outrank likes in the For You feed ranking.
- The For You tab leans on creator verification signals and Community Notes history as quality filters.
- Engagement velocity in the first two hours determines amplification into non-follower feeds.

<Callout type="tip">
Posts that earn comments in the first 60 minutes receive 2.1x more total engagement on average, per 2025 platform data. Schedule your posting time around when you can respond to early comments, not just when your audience is online.
</Callout>

## Real Company Examples

### Duolingo on TikTok: Engineering for Completion Rate

Duolingo grew its TikTok following from roughly 50,000 in mid-2021 to over 15 million by 2025 without a traditional paid media push. The social team's strategy was not "go viral." It was systematic: every video was designed around the one signal TikTok weights most heavily. Short, looping, audio-on jokes featuring the brand mascot pushed completion rates above 100% (users rewatching the loop), which triggered repeated placement in the For You Page algorithm. The result was organic reach that would cost millions to replicate through paid channels.

### LinkedIn Thought Leaders: Dwell Time as Content Design Principle

Several B2B consultants and SaaS companies reported 3x to 5x reach increases in 2025 after switching from link-heavy posts to long-form native text posts, specifically because LinkedIn's 360Brew model deprioritizes external links (they send users off-platform, reducing dwell time). One documented case from Postiv AI's 2026 analysis showed a SaaS founder whose native carousel posts consistently reached 40,000 to 80,000 impressions per post, while identical content posted as an external blog link averaged under 3,000. The only variable was whether the content stayed on LinkedIn or redirected users away.

<Callout type="example">
Meta's RankNet-7 AI, deployed across Facebook and Instagram, reduced content abandonment by 27.4% by learning to deprioritize posts that users scroll past quickly. This means every scroll-past from a disinterested user actively hurts a post's ranking. Targeting a narrower, more relevant audience often outperforms broadcasting to a wide one, because lower skip rates send stronger quality signals to the algorithm.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes

- **Treating all platforms as the same distribution channel.** A LinkedIn carousel optimized for 3 minutes of dwell time will destroy your TikTok completion rate. Build separate content for each platform's dominant signal.
- **Chasing likes.** Likes are the weakest signal on every major platform. Saves, shares, sends, and substantive comments are what actually move ranking. A post with 10 saves outperforms one with 100 likes on most platforms.
- **Posting and ghosting.** On LinkedIn and Instagram, failing to engage with early comments signals low-quality content to the algorithm. Replying within the first 60 minutes is a ranking input, not just courtesy.
- **Ignoring the first 2 to 3 seconds.** On TikTok and Instagram Reels, drop-off in the opening seconds is the single biggest predictor of throttled distribution. Your hook is not creative flair; it is a technical requirement.
- **Using engagement pods or buying followers.** Modern models like LinkedIn's 360Brew and Meta's RankNet-7 detect coordinated inauthentic behavior patterns and down-rank the account at the account level, not just the individual post. Recovery from a pod penalty can take months.
- **Posting external links on LinkedIn.** LinkedIn's algorithm explicitly deprioritizes posts that send users off-platform, because they reduce dwell time on LinkedIn. If you need to share a link, put it in the first comment, not the post body.

## Key Takeaways

- Every major feed is now an interest graph. Each post earns its reach individually, based on predicted value to the viewer, not based on who follows you.
- AI drives 80%+ of content recommendations across platforms. You are optimizing for a machine learning model, not a human editor.
- One dominant signal per platform: completion rate (TikTok), sends and saves (Instagram Reels), dwell time plus comment quality (LinkedIn), AVD plus CTR (YouTube).
- Saves and shares outweigh likes on every major platform as of 2025. Design content for "save this for later" rather than "tap the heart."
- The first 60 minutes after posting is a diagnostic window. Early engagement velocity, especially replies and comments, determines whether the algorithm extends reach to a second-tier audience.
- Format matters as much as content. Native formats (carousels on LinkedIn, Reels on Instagram, Shorts on YouTube) get preferential ranking because they maximize time on platform.
