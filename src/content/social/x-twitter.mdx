# X (Twitter) Playbook

X is no longer the broadcast megaphone it was in 2015 — it is a real-time conversation graph wrapped around tightly-clustered niche audiences (tech, finance, sports, politics, crypto, gaming). If your buyers live in one of those tribes, X is still one of the highest-leverage owned-media channels on the internet. This playbook is for marketers who want to build an audience that compounds — not chase virality.

## What It Actually Is

X is a short-form, public, reverse-chronological-ish feed where posts ("tweets") are surfaced by an algorithm that heavily weights replies, bookmarks, and time-on-post. Concretely: when Notion's account quote-posts a customer's screenshot at 9:42 a.m., gets 40 replies in 20 minutes, and 200 bookmarks, the algorithm pushes that post into the For You feed of users who follow productivity accounts. That is the whole game — write things people want to react to or save.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The audience is smaller than it was, but the buyer concentration is unusually high:

- X reached roughly **611 million monthly active users in 2025**, up from 600 million in 2024, per [Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/twitter-statistics/).
- **79% of users follow brands** for updates and **37.9% use X to research products** — and users are roughly **2x more likely to make in-app purchases** than on other platforms ([Sprout Social, 2025](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/twitter-statistics/)).
- Average influencer engagement rate jumped from **0.09% in 2024 to 0.39% in 2025**, with text-only posts performing best at **0.48%** ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/twitter-statistics/)).
- X's 2024 ad revenue was an estimated **$1.94 billion** — small compared to Meta — but advertisers report a **40% higher ROI** than other platforms on average ([Business of Apps, 2025](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/twitter-statistics/)).

Translation: lower reach, higher intent. The people who are still here are *very* online and they buy.

## How It Works / The Playbook

1. **Pick one niche and one POV.** "Marketing tips" loses. "How early-stage SaaS founders should think about pricing" wins. The algorithm rewards topical consistency because it improves For You targeting.
2. **Post 3–5x per day, mostly text.** Text-only posts beat images and links in 2025. Save links for replies, not the main post — links suppress reach.
3. **Reply more than you post.** Spend 20–30 minutes a day replying with substance to accounts 2–3 tiers larger than yours. This is how you steal their audience without paying for ads.
4. **Use the "hook, body, payoff" structure** for longer posts (X now allows up to 25,000 characters for Premium users): one-line hook, scannable body with line breaks, payoff or question at the end.
5. **Bookmarks > likes.** Optimize for save-worthy content (checklists, frameworks, screenshots of useful UI). Bookmarks are the strongest ranking signal after replies.
6. **Quote-post customers and creators.** It is the fastest way to get reciprocal distribution and warm intros.
7. **Run X Ads only after organic works.** Use Follower Ads to scale an account that already has a 1%+ engagement rate. Promoting weak content just lights money on fire.

<Callout type="example">
Wendy's built a roughly 4-million-follower account on X almost entirely through reply-game and roasts — no paid promotion of the handle itself. Their 2017 "Nuggs for Carter" reply (asking @carterjwm how many retweets for a year of free nuggets) generated **3.43 million retweets**, became the most-retweeted post in history at the time, and drove an estimated **$5M+ in earned media** per Marketing Brew. The mechanic still works in 2025: pick a brand voice that can credibly reply to anyone, then actually reply.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes

- **Treating X like LinkedIn.** Long, polished, corporate-voiced posts die. Lowercase, opinionated, slightly-too-honest posts win.
- **Posting links in the main tweet.** The algorithm demotes external links. Post the hook, drop the link in the first reply.
- **Chasing virality instead of niche depth.** A viral post from outside your niche brings followers who never buy and tank future engagement rates.
- **Ignoring replies for 24 hours.** Most algorithmic lift happens in the first 30–60 minutes. If you post and ghost, you lose the window.
- **Buying followers or engagement pods.** X's 2024 algorithm update actively penalizes accounts with mismatched follower-to-engagement ratios.

## Key Takeaways

- X in 2025 is a niche-tribe platform with high purchase intent, not a mass-reach channel — pick a tribe and commit.
- Text-only posts, replies, and bookmark-bait outperform images, links, and threads on raw engagement.
- The first hour after posting decides the next 24 hours of reach — be present, reply to every reply, and pin the best one.
