# Instagram Playbook

Instagram in 2025 is not the photo-grid app you grew up on. Engagement is down roughly 24% year-over-year, Reels are table stakes rather than a growth hack, and the algorithm now weights DM sends heavier than likes. This playbook is for marketers and creators who need to ship content that actually reaches people  -  not just publish into the void.

## What It Actually Is

Instagram is now a discovery engine wrapped around three formats: Reels (short vertical video), carousels (multi-slide swipe posts, up to 20 slides), and Stories (24-hour ephemeral slides). Each format does a different job. Example: Duolingo uses Reels for new-audience reach (the owl trend videos), carousels for educational depth ("5 ways to say hello in Japanese"), and Stories for polls and launch announcements to the existing fanbase.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The format math has flipped. Carousel posts now hit a 10.15% average engagement rate, beating single-image posts (~7%) and Reels (~6%), per [ReelBase's 2025 analysis](https://reelbase.io/blog/instagram-carousel-reels-complete-guide). Carousels also pull 1.4x wider reach and 3.1x higher engagement than single photos.

Reels still win for discovery: average Reels engagement sits at 1.23% vs 0.70% for image posts, and Reels up to 3 minutes are now eligible for the Explore page, per [Buffer's 2026 algorithm guide](https://buffer.com/resources/instagram-algorithms/). Adam Mosseri has confirmed that watch time, likes, and sends are the three heaviest signals  -  and a DM send counts more than a like when Instagram decides whether to push your post to non-followers, according to [Later's algorithm breakdown](https://later.com/blog/how-instagram-algorithm-works/).

Meanwhile, [Social Insider's benchmarks](https://www.socialinsider.io/social-media-benchmarks/instagram) show overall engagement down ~24% YoY in 2025  -  the bar for "good" has dropped, but so has organic reach. You have to work harder for the same numbers.

## How It Works / The Playbook

**Reels  -  for reach:**
1. Hook in the first 3 seconds. Show the payoff, then earn the watch.
2. Cut every 1–2 seconds. Pattern interrupts keep retention above the 50% threshold that triggers algorithmic boosts.
3. Keep it under 90 seconds. Instagram openly favors shorter Reels.
4. Use native trending sounds, but add keyword-rich on-screen captions so the post is searchable.
5. End with a loop or a question that drives a comment or share.

**Carousels  -  for trust and saves:**
1. Cover slide = plain-language promise ("7 ways to lower your CAC"). No clever copy.
2. 6–10 slides. Mixed media (image + video slides) hits 2.33% engagement vs 1.80% for image-only.
3. Each slide should provoke the next swipe  -  cliffhanger, numbered step, or visual continuation.
4. End with a saveable recap slide. Saves are a strong ranking signal.

**Stories  -  for the existing audience:**
1. 6–13 slides per sequence; front-load the context on slide 1.
2. Mix in a poll, quiz, or slider sticker every 3–4 slides to spike completion rate.
3. Use link stickers with UTM tags so you can measure traffic in GA4.

## Common Mistakes

- Posting Reels without captions. Most viewers watch on mute  -  no captions means no retention.
- Using a clever or cryptic cover slide on carousels. Plain English wins; curiosity loses to clarity.
- Optimizing for likes when sends and saves are the signals that matter for reach.
- Treating Stories as a dumping ground. A 20-slide unstructured Story has worse completion than a tight 8-slide sequence with a poll.
- Chasing trending audio that has no connection to your message. The algorithm rewards watch time, not audio choice.

## Key Takeaways

- Carousels are the highest-engagement format in 2025  -  use them for authority and saves.
- Reels are how you reach non-followers, but they no longer guarantee growth. Hook hard, cut fast, caption everything.
- Sends and saves outweigh likes in the algorithm. Design content that's worth DMing to a friend.
