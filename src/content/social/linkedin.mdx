# LinkedIn Playbook

LinkedIn quietly became the most valuable distribution channel in B2B. If you sell to professionals, hire engineers, or build a category, your founders, execs, and product marketers should be publishing here weekly. This playbook is for B2B operators, founder-led marketers, and anyone trying to compress a 9-month sales cycle into a warm inbound DM.

## What It Actually Is

LinkedIn is two products glued together: a professional identity graph (resumes, companies, connections) and a content feed ranked by an interest-and-engagement algorithm. The opportunity is that the feed is dominated by personal profiles, not company pages, so a single human at your company can outperform your brand handle by 10x with the same content. A concrete example: a founder posting a 200-word breakdown of how they priced their SaaS will routinely beat the same story published from the company page.

## Why It Matters (with data)

- 76% of B2B marketers say LinkedIn is the single most effective channel for thought leadership, per the [2025 Edelman-LinkedIn B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report](https://www.edelman.com/expertise/Business-Marketing/2025-b2b-thought-leadership-report).
- LinkedIn Premium subscribers grew 50% between 2023 and 2025, and Premium revenue crossed $2B annually in Q2 2025 ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/linkedin-statistics/)). Translation: the audience is paying to be there, which means they show up.
- Video posts receive roughly 5x more engagement than text-only posts, and LinkedIn's 2024-2025 algorithm explicitly down-weights corporate broadcasting in favor of authentic personal posts ([Buffer's LinkedIn algorithm breakdown](https://buffer.com/resources/linkedin-algorithm/)).
- The same Edelman report found that a majority of decision-makers are "hidden buyers" who consume thought leadership for months before ever filling out a form. If you only measure MQLs, you will misread the channel.

## How It Works / The Playbook

The algorithm ranks on three signals: **relevance** (does the post match this viewer's interest graph), **expertise** (do you consistently post about one topic), and **engagement** (especially comments in the first 60-90 minutes). Optimize for those, not for likes.

1. **Pick one narrow lane.** Not "marketing." Pick "pricing for vertical SaaS" or "demand gen for cybersecurity." The expertise signal compounds when you stay on topic.
2. **Post 3-5 times per week from a personal profile**, not the company page. Personal posts reach more feeds with identical content.
3. **Use the hook-and-whitespace format.** The first two lines decide whether someone clicks "see more." Lead with a number, a contradiction, or a confession. Use short paragraphs because 91%+ of viewing is mobile.
4. **Put links in the first comment, not the post body.** Outbound-link posts get throttled.
5. **Reply to every comment in the first two hours.** Each reply re-triggers distribution and signals to the algorithm that the post is worth promoting.
6. **Comment more than you post.** For accounts under 5,000 followers, thoughtful 2-3 sentence comments on bigger creators' posts will grow you faster than your own content.
7. **Mix formats weekly:** one text story, one carousel (PDF document post), one short native video, one data/chart post.
8. **Use Sales Navigator + warm DMs as the bottom of the funnel.** Content earns the right to send the DM.

## Common Mistakes

- **Posting from the company page only.** It is the lowest-reach surface on the platform. Use it for credentialing, not distribution.
- **Engagement bait.** "Agree? Comment YES below." The 2024 algorithm update explicitly demotes this and can suppress your next several posts.
- **Putting the link in the post body.** Move it to the first comment and add "link in comments" to the body.
- **Topic drift.** Posting about parenting one day, AI the next, and SOC 2 the third trains the algorithm to show your content to no one in particular.
- **Ghosting your own comment section.** If you do not reply within 2 hours, you forfeit most of the distribution boost.
- **Measuring likes instead of qualified DMs and replies.** The hidden-buyer dynamic means real ROI shows up in inbound, not in vanity metrics.

## Key Takeaways

- Distribution lives on personal profiles; use the company page for proof, not reach.
- Pick one narrow expertise lane and stay in it for at least 90 days before judging results.
- Optimize the first 90 minutes after posting: hook, comments in body, you replying to every comment.
- Track inbound DMs, demo requests citing a post, and sales-cycle compression — not likes.
