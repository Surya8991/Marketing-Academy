# AI for SEO

SEO in 2025 is not about stuffing a page with one keyword anymore. Google's AI Overviews and the shift to semantic search reward sites that cover whole topics, link them tightly, and answer real intent. AI tools let a one-person content team do the keyword clustering, brief writing, and internal link mapping that used to take an agency.

## What It Actually Is

AI for SEO means using large language models and embeddings to do three jobs that used to be manual: group thousands of keywords into intent clusters, turn each cluster into a structured content brief, and map internal links between related pages on your site. Example: paste 2,000 keywords from Google Search Console into a tool like Keyword Insights, and it returns 80 topic clusters, each with a suggested pillar page, supporting articles, and the SERP intent (informational, commercial, transactional).

## How It Works / The Playbook

Run this three-stage workflow once a quarter for each content pillar:

1. **Cluster your keywords by intent.** Export 1,000-5,000 keywords from Google Search Console, Ahrefs, or Semrush. Feed them to a clustering tool (Keyword Insights, SEOcluster.ai, or a custom script using OpenAI embeddings + HDBSCAN). The tool groups keywords whose SERPs overlap, which is a stronger signal than semantic similarity alone.
2. **Generate content briefs per cluster.** For each cluster, prompt an LLM with the top 10 ranking URLs, the People Also Ask questions, and the cluster keywords. Ask for: H2/H3 outline, target word count, entities to mention, FAQ section, and the search intent in one sentence. Frase, Surfer, and Keyword Insights automate this end-to-end.
3. **Map internal links with embeddings.** Crawl your site, embed each URL's content, then for every new article find the top 5 most semantically similar existing pages and propose anchor text. This is where tools like seo.ai's internal linking tool or a custom Pinecone + GPT pipeline beat manual linking by an order of magnitude.

## Common Mistakes

- Clustering on semantic similarity only, ignoring SERP overlap. Two keywords can mean the same thing but rank different page types, so they need separate articles.
- Publishing AI-generated briefs as finished articles. The brief is scaffolding, not the building. Human editing, original data, and expert quotes are what beat the 10 pages already ranking.
- Internal-linking every new article to the same 5 "money pages." Embeddings-based linking spreads equity to deep pages and lifts long-tail rankings, which is where most organic traffic actually comes from.
- Treating AI Overviews as a threat instead of a format. Pages structured around clear questions, short answers, and entity-rich content get cited inside the Overview block.

## Key Takeaways

- Cluster keywords by SERP overlap, not just meaning, before you write anything.
- Use AI to produce the brief and the link map, not the final prose. Editorial judgment is still the moat.
- Internal linking driven by embeddings is the highest-ROI AI SEO play most sites are not yet running.
