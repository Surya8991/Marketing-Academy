## Step 1, Audience Research with AI

Before writing a single line of copy, use AI to map your audience's real language.

**Prompt template for audience research:**

```
You are a paid media strategist. I am advertising [product] to [audience].

List:
1. The top 5 fears this audience has related to [problem your product solves]
2. The top 5 desires they openly express
3. 3 objections they use to avoid buying
4. Exact phrases they use in reviews, forums, or social posts

Format as a table.
```

Feed the output directly into your copy. Real audience language outperforms polished brand language in ad creative every time.

**For Google Ads keyword research**, use tools like Semrush's AI keyword clustering or Google's own AI-powered Search term reports to find converting query patterns, not just volume.

## Step 2, The 4-Concept Creative Matrix

Most AI copy tools produce generic output because they receive generic input. Fix this with the creative matrix method.

| Concept Angle | Static Image | Short Video | Carousel | UGC-Style |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fear (loss) | Variant 1 | Variant 2 | Variant 3 | Variant 4 |
| Desire (gain) | Variant 5 | Variant 6 | Variant 7 | Variant 8 |
| Social proof | Variant 9 | Variant 10 | Variant 11 | Variant 12 |
| Curiosity | Variant 13 | Variant 14 | Variant 15 | Variant 16 |

Start with four distinct emotional angles. For each angle, produce one version per format. That gives you 16 variants from a single brief. AI tools handle the copy variation; your creative director approves tone and brand fit.

<Callout type="example">
A DTC skincare brand used this matrix to generate 64 Meta ad variants in one sprint. Within two weeks of testing, three variants (all in the "desire + UGC-style" cell) drove 80% of conversions at a CPA 38% below their manual campaign baseline.
</Callout>

## Step 3, AI Copy Generation Tools

### For Meta and Google Ad Copy

**ChatGPT / Claude**, Best for first-draft copy at scale. Use specific persona prompts, not generic "write an ad" requests.

**Jasper Ads**, Purpose-built with platform-specific templates for Facebook, Google, LinkedIn.

**AdCreative.ai**, Generates full visual creative plus copy, trained on high-performing ad data.

**Copy.ai Workflows**, Automates multi-step brief-to-copy pipelines with brand voice guardrails.

### Prompt Structure That Works

```
Platform: [Facebook Feed / Google Search / LinkedIn Sponsored]
Audience: [specific persona description]
Product: [name + one-line description]
Goal: [awareness / clicks / conversions]
Tone: [3 adjectives]
Constraint: [max characters or word count]
Angle: [fear / desire / proof / curiosity]

Write 5 distinct headline + body copy combinations.
Flag which psychological trigger each uses.
```

## Step 4, Platform AI: Performance Max and Advantage+

Platform AI handles bidding, placement, and audience expansion automatically. But it only performs well when you give it strong creative inputs.

### Google Performance Max

Performance Max (PMax) consolidates all Google inventory, Search, Display, YouTube, Discover, Maps, Gmail, into one campaign type driven by AI optimization.

**What you must provide:**
- At least 5 headlines (up to 15), at least 5 descriptions (up to 4)
- 3-5 images in multiple aspect ratios
- 1-2 video assets (Google will auto-generate video if you skip this, and it will be generic)
- A clear conversion goal with enough historical data

**Critical rule:** give PMax your best creative inputs from the matrix exercise above. The AI optimizes delivery; it cannot fix weak creative.

<Callout type="example">
L'Oreal ran Performance Max campaigns across 12 product lines. With AI-generated creative variants fed into PMax, they achieved a 2x conversion rate and 31% lower CPA compared to their previous Standard Shopping campaigns. Renault Mais used PMax for vehicle lead generation and cut CPA by 86% versus their prior display campaigns. HRC Fertility generated leads at 4x lower cost than previous Search-only campaigns after switching to PMax with video assets.
</Callout>

### Meta Advantage+

Meta's Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns (ASC) work similarly: you provide creative, Meta's AI finds the audience and placement.

**Setup checklist:**
- Upload 10-20 creative variations (Advantage+ tests them automatically)
- Enable Advantage+ audience (removes manual targeting restrictions)
- Set a daily budget high enough to exit the learning phase within 7 days
- Connect your product catalog for dynamic creative

<Callout type="tip">
The learning phase requires roughly 50 conversion events in 7 days. If your daily budget cannot generate that volume, the algorithm never optimizes. A common mistake is launching with a $20/day budget for a $200 product and expecting AI magic. Fund the learning phase properly or the AI has nothing to learn from.
</Callout>

## Step 5, Bid Strategy and Budget Automation

Manual bidding is no longer competitive at scale. Platform AI handles micro-adjustments across thousands of signals, device, time, location, user history, auction competition, faster than any human can.

**Recommended bid strategies by goal:**

| Goal | Google Strategy | Meta Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Conversions at target CPA | Target CPA | Cost per result goal |
| Maximize ROAS | Target ROAS | ROAS goal (ASC) |
| Scale volume | Maximize conversions | Highest volume |
| New product launch | Maximize clicks then switch | Awareness then conversion |

For new campaigns with no conversion history, start with Maximize Clicks (Google) or Highest Volume (Meta) for 2-3 weeks to build the data signal. Then switch to a value-based strategy.

**Third-party AI budget tools:** Revealbot, Madgicx, and Optmyzr automate budget reallocation between ad sets based on real-time performance. These are useful when managing 10+ active campaigns simultaneously.

## Step 6, AI-Assisted Creative Refresh Cycle

Creative fatigue is the top reason ROAS declines after week 3-4. Set a calendar trigger.

**Frequency by channel:**
- Meta: refresh creative every 3-4 weeks or when frequency exceeds 3.0
- Google Display: refresh every 4-6 weeks or when CTR drops more than 20%
- YouTube: refresh every 6-8 weeks or when view-through rate drops 15%

Use AI to generate the next batch before performance drops. Do not wait for the data to crater.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1, Letting the platform generate creative for you

Both Google and Meta will auto-generate assets if you do not provide them. Google's auto-generated video for PMax is generic and low-quality. Always provide your own video assets.

### Mistake 2, Too few creative variants

Advantage+ and PMax need variety to optimize. Uploading 2-3 creatives gives the AI almost nothing to work with. Aim for at least 10 distinct creatives per campaign.

### Mistake 3, Underfunding the learning phase

The single most common reason AI campaigns underperform. If your budget cannot generate 50 conversions in 7 days at your target CPA, double the budget for the first two weeks or the algorithm learns nothing.

### Mistake 4, Ignoring the creative brief

AI copy tools produce generic output from generic input. Every prompt needs a specific audience, a specific emotional angle, and a specific constraint. "Write an ad for my product" wastes the tool.

### Mistake 5, No human review loop

AI does not know your brand's competitive positioning, current PR sensitivities, or product nuances. Build a review step before any creative goes live. Assign a human to check for tone, accuracy, and brand safety. This is not optional.
