# Growth Loops

If your weekly review still asks 'how many people came in the top of the funnel?', you are measuring a leaky bucket. The fastest-growing products in 2024 and 2025 — Canva, TikTok, Pinterest, Notion — do not pour water into a bucket. They build engines where last week's users produce next week's users. This lesson is for growth marketers and PMs ready to retire the linear funnel and design systems that compound.

## What It Actually Is

A growth loop is a closed system where the output of one cycle becomes the input of the next. One cohort of users directly creates the next cohort, with no fresh marketing spend required to keep it spinning.

Concrete example: a Canva user designs a poster and exports it with a small 'Made with Canva' watermark. That poster gets shared on Instagram. Two viewers click through. One signs up, designs their own poster, and the loop repeats. Output (designs) becomes input (new signups) becomes more output.

The four core loop types you will encounter:

1. **Viral / user-generated content loops** — users create content that attracts more users (Canva, TikTok)
2. **Paid loops** — revenue from one cohort funds acquisition of the next (Booking.com, HelloFresh)
3. **Content / SEO loops** — users or the product generate indexable pages that rank and attract more users (Pinterest, Glassdoor, Reddit)
4. **Sales-assisted loops** — customers refer or expand within their organization (Slack, Notion)

## Why It Matters (with data)

Brian Balfour's team at Reforge made the original argument plainly: funnels are 'too micro a view to answer how your product grows' and they treat product, acquisition, and monetization in silos. Loops force you to think about them as one system. [Growth Loops are the New Funnels — Reforge](https://www.reforge.com/blog/growth-loops).

The compounding math is the point. Reforge contrasts a campaign delivering 500 new users once against a loop delivering 20 users in week one and growing 10% week-over-week. By week 52, the linear campaign is dead and the loop is producing thousands per week. [Ultimate Guide: Growth Loops — Aakash Gupta](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/ultimate-guide-growth-loops).

Real numbers from 2025: Canva crossed 220 million monthly active users in early 2025, with the vast majority arriving through user-shared designs, paid contributors, and an affiliate program where referrers earn on every signup — three interlocking loops, not a single funnel. [Canva Marketing Strategy 2025 — Latterly](https://www.latterly.org/canva-marketing-strategy/).

## How It Works / The Playbook

Diagram any candidate loop in four parts before building anything:

1. **Input** — what triggers the loop? (a new user, a piece of content, a dollar of revenue)
2. **Action** — what does that input do inside your product?
3. **Output** — what artifact, signal, or asset is produced?
4. **Re-investment** — how does the output become a fresh input?

Then quantify each step:

- For every 100 inputs, how many complete the action? (Conversion)
- For every 100 actions, how many produce a re-investable output? (Yield)
- For every 100 outputs, how many become a new input? (Loop velocity)

Multiply the three. If your loop factor is below 1.0, it leaks faster than it compounds — you have a funnel pretending to be a loop. Above 1.0, it grows on its own.

## Common Mistakes

- **Calling a referral program a loop when it is a campaign.** If the referral only fires once per user and offers a one-time reward, it is a funnel with extra steps. A real loop has continuous re-investment.
- **Optimizing the funnel inside the loop and ignoring the loop itself.** You can double signup conversion and still have a loop factor under 1.0. Measure the whole cycle, not just one step.
- **Building three loops at once.** Early-stage teams almost always lose. Pick one loop, get it to a factor above 1.0, then layer a second.
- **Ignoring decay.** Loops degrade — algorithms change, channels saturate, content gets stale. A loop that worked in 2023 may be flat in 2025. Reforecast quarterly.

## Key Takeaways

- Funnels are linear and finite. Loops are systems where output becomes input, enabling exponential rather than linear growth.
- The job is not to find a magic loop — it is to map your candidate loop into input, action, output, re-investment, then measure each step honestly.
- A loop factor below 1.0 is a leaky funnel in disguise. Above 1.0 is a compounding asset.
- Canva, TikTok, and Pinterest did not win by spending more on ads. They won by designing the product so users produce the next cohort of users.
