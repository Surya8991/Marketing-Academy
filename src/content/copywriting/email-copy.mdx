# Email Copywriting

Email generates $36 for every $1 spent, a 3,500% ROI that outperforms Google Ads and social media combined. Yet most marketers lose that return in the first 47 characters of a subject line.

## Quick Summary

- Email copywriting is a four-part chain: subject line, preheader, body, and CTA. Each part has one job.
- 47% of recipients decide whether to open an email based solely on the subject line.
- Personalized subject lines lift open rates by 26% and reply rates by 133%.
- A single, focused CTA generates 371% more clicks than emails with multiple CTAs.
- Post-purchase emails average a 76.58% open rate, showing that context and timing matter as much as copy.

## What It Actually Is

Email copywriting is the craft of writing the words inside a marketing email so that each section earns the next action from the reader. It is not one block of prose. It is a relay race where each runner must hand the baton cleanly to the next.

Think of it like a job interview. The subject line gets you in the door. The preheader is the handshake. The body is the actual interview. The CTA is asking for the job. If any step fails, the whole sequence collapses, regardless of how strong the rest is.

## Why It Matters (with data)

Email is the highest-ROI channel in digital marketing, and the numbers from 2025 benchmarks are striking:

- The global average email open rate in 2025 is **42.35%**, per [MailerLite's 2025 benchmarks](https://www.mailerlite.com/blog/email-marketing-statistics).
- The average click rate sits at **2%**, but personalized campaigns show a **267% higher CTR** than non-personalized ones.
- There are **4.37 billion global email users** as of 2025. Even a 1% improvement in your open rate scales to enormous reach.
- Subject lines in the 41-50 character range achieve the highest CTR at **17.57%**, per [Belkins' 2025 subject line study](https://belkins.io/blog/b2b-cold-email-subject-line-statistics).
- CTA buttons outperform text links by up to **127% in CTR**, according to Campaign Monitor research.
- Emails with a **single CTA** generate 371% more clicks than emails with multiple competing CTAs, per [Moosend's CTA research](https://moosend.com/blog/email-cta/).

Bad email copy wastes the list you spent months building. Strong copy treats that list as a compounding asset, where every send teaches you something about what your audience actually wants.

## How It Works: The Playbook

The four-part structure works in strict sequence. Each component has exactly one job.

### 1. Subject Line

The subject line earns the open. Nothing else matters until this works.

- Keep it between 41-50 characters for highest CTR. Under 70 to avoid mobile truncation.
- Use the word "you" or reference something specific to the reader.
- Question-format subject lines hit a **46% open rate**, higher than any other format.
- Personalized subject lines generate a 46% open rate vs. 35% without personalization.
- Avoid all-caps and multiple exclamation marks. Spam filters flag them. Readers ignore them.
- Write the subject line last, after you know exactly what the email delivers.

### 2. Preheader Text

The preheader is the line of text shown beside the subject line in the inbox preview. Most marketers leave it blank or let the email client auto-pull the first line of body copy.

- Write it intentionally, every time.
- Treat it as a second subject line, not a repeat of the first.
- Add a new reason to open: if the subject line says "Your free guide is ready," the preheader says "3 frameworks you can use today."
- Keep it under 90 characters. Some clients cut it earlier.

### 3. Body Copy

The body builds the case for clicking. One case, not five.

- Open with your most important sentence. Readers scan before they commit to reading.
- Use short paragraphs: two to three sentences maximum. Long paragraphs signal effort for the reader.
- Write to one person. "You" should appear more than "we."
- Make one argument. If you have three value propositions, write three emails.
- Use plain language. If a 12-year-old would not understand the sentence, rewrite it.
- Mirror the subject line's tone. If the subject line was casual, the body cannot suddenly be formal.

### 4. CTA Button

The CTA is the only thing you actually want the reader to do. Treat it accordingly.

- Use action verbs: "Get," "Start," "Download," "Book," "Claim."
- Add specificity: "Get my free guide" beats "Download now." First-person language ("my" instead of "your") consistently outperforms in A/B tests.
- Place the CTA above the fold. Do not make the reader scroll to find the next step.
- Use one CTA per email. Multiple CTAs split attention and reduce total clicks by up to 371%.
- Button CTAs outperform text links by 127% in CTR. Use an actual button, not a hyperlink.

<Callout type="tip">
**The preheader is your most underused asset.** Most inboxes display 40-90 characters of preheader text right next to the subject line. That is free real estate most senders waste. Write it as if it were a second subject line with a different angle on why this email is worth opening right now.
</Callout>

## Real Company Examples

### Grammarly Reactivation Campaign (2023)

Grammarly targeted lapsed free-tier users with a subject line: "You have unused writing suggestions." It referenced something the user had already done, their own drafts, without feeling invasive. The preheader read: "Pick up where you left off." The body was three sentences. The CTA said "See my suggestions" in first person. The campaign achieved a 42% open rate against a 2025 industry average of 42.35%, which is notable for a reactivation sequence targeting cold users who had already disengaged.

<Callout type="example">
**What made it work:** The subject line used the reader's own data as the hook, not Grammarly's features. The preheader extended the thought rather than repeating it. The CTA used "my" instead of "your." Every element pointed at the reader, not the product. This is the hallmark of high-performing email copy: the reader feels like the email is about them, not about the company sending it.
</Callout>

### Airbnb Booking Confirmation Sequence

Airbnb's post-booking emails consistently benchmark above the industry average because they exploit timing. A booking confirmation email arrives when the reader is at peak emotional investment. Airbnb uses that moment to cross-sell experiences, travel insurance, and local recommendations with CTAs that feel like helpful next steps rather than upsells. The subject lines are specific ("Your stay in Lisbon is confirmed") and the CTAs extend the journey ("Explore things to do in Lisbon"). Post-purchase automation emails across the industry average a **76.58% open rate and 28.43% click rate** per MailerLite 2025 data, proving that timing and relevance beat clever writing every time.

<Callout type="example">
**What made it work:** Airbnb treats the confirmation email as the opening of a conversation, not the close of a transaction. The subject line anchors to something specific (the destination). The body is short. The CTAs feel like service, not selling. When the email arrives at the right moment with the right context, the copy does not have to work as hard.
</Callout>

### Duolingo Win-Back Email (2024)

Duolingo's win-back emails use their mascot, Duo the owl, with guilt-inducing subject lines like "We miss you" or "Duo is waiting..." The approach is deliberately emotional rather than feature-focused. The body copy is often a single sentence. The CTA is "Keep your streak." The gamification language ("streak") is specific to Duolingo's product and triggers a loss-aversion response in users who had streaks before. Win-back emails that reference a specific behavior the user stopped doing consistently outperform generic "we miss you" messages with no behavioral hook.

## Common Mistakes

**1. Writing the subject line first**
Most writers draft the subject line before the email exists. Then they have to shoehorn the body copy into matching something they already committed to. Write the email first. Write the subject line last, when you know exactly what promise to make.

**2. Letting the preheader default**
If you do not write a preheader, the email client pulls the first line of body copy. That first line is often "View this email in your browser" or the legal footer. You are handing free inbox real estate to your unsubscribe link.

**3. Multiple CTAs competing for attention**
Adding a secondary CTA ("Or, follow us on Instagram") splits the reader's attention. Emails with a single CTA generate 371% more clicks. Every additional option reduces the chance the reader takes any action at all.

**4. Writing "we" more than "you"**
Phrases like "We are excited to announce" and "Our team has built" center the sender. Readers care about what is in it for them. Audit any email you write: count "we/our" and count "you/your." If "we" wins, rewrite.

**5. Vague CTA copy**
"Click here," "Learn more," and "Submit" are the three weakest CTAs in email. They describe an action without naming the reward. Replace every vague CTA with the specific outcome: "Get the free template," "Start my 14-day trial," "Book a 20-minute demo."

**6. Mismatching subject line and body**
If the subject line promises something the email does not deliver, readers feel deceived. Open rates go up temporarily. Unsubscribes and spam reports follow. Keep the promise the subject line makes.

<Callout type="warning">
**The bait-and-switch subject line is the fastest way to destroy a list.** A subject line like "Your account has been suspended" when nothing has actually happened will get opens. It will also generate spam complaints and damage your sender reputation permanently. Inbox providers track complaint rates. Once your domain lands in spam folders, no amount of copy quality will save your deliverability.
</Callout>

## Key Takeaways

- Email returns $36 for every $1 spent. Most of that return is determined by copy quality, not list size.
- 47% of recipients open or skip based solely on the subject line. Write it last, when you know exactly what the email delivers.
- Personalized subject lines lift open rates 26% and reply rates 133%. Specificity is the mechanism.
- One CTA per email. Every additional option you add reduces total clicks.
- The preheader is free advertising space. Almost no one writes it intentionally. That is your edge.
- Post-purchase and behavioral trigger emails average 76%+ open rates. Timing beats copywriting every time. Use both.
