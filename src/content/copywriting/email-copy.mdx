## Subject Lines: The Gate to Everything

Your subject line determines whether the rest of your email copy matters at all. Belkins' 2025 cold email study found that subject lines between 41 and 50 characters achieved a 17.57% CTR, significantly above shorter or longer alternatives.

### Formulas that work

**The curiosity gap:** "What 94% of marketers get wrong about email"

**The specific number:** "3 subject line mistakes killing your open rates"

**The question:** "Are you leaving $36 per subscriber on the table?" (Question-format subject lines hit 46% open rates per Belkins 2025)

**The direct benefit:** "Double your click rate in 7 days"

**The personal:** "Surya, your campaign has a blind spot"

<Callout type="warning">
Avoid subject line tricks like ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation marks, or misleading "Re:" prefixes. They spike opens once, then destroy trust and future deliverability.
</Callout>

### Preview text: the second subject line

Preview text is the grey snippet shown next to the subject line in most email clients. Most marketers ignore it. Treat it as a second subject line that continues the curiosity or benefit started in the first.

**Subject:** "3 mistakes killing your open rates"
**Preview:** "Number 2 is the one nobody talks about"

## Opening Lines: Earn the Read

The first sentence must pull the reader into the second sentence. It cannot start with "I" or your company name. It must start with the reader's world.

**Weak opening:** "Hi, I'm writing to share our latest feature update..."

**Strong opening:** "Your welcome email sequence is losing 40% of new subscribers before they ever see your best content."

The strong version opens with a specific, credible problem that the reader cares about. It creates tension that the rest of the email resolves.

<Callout type="example">
**Grammarly (2023 re-engagement campaign):** Their most successful reactivation email opened with: "You used to write 3x more with Grammarly. Here is what changed." That single line referenced personal data, created curiosity, and implied loss, all without a single sales word. The campaign recovered 18% of dormant users.
</Callout>

## Body Copy: Build Desire Without Friction

### Write to one person

Even if you are emailing 500,000 subscribers, write as if you are writing to one specific person. Use "you" more than "we." Keep sentences short. Keep paragraphs to three lines maximum for mobile readability.

### The PAS framework

**Problem:** Name the pain precisely. Vague problems create vague desire.
**Agitation:** Make the problem feel urgent and real. What does it cost them if they ignore it?
**Solution:** Introduce your offer as the natural resolution.

### The personalization multiplier

Campaign Monitor data shows personalized emails generate 26% higher open rates. But personalization is not just first names. It includes:

- Behavior-based triggers ("You viewed X but did not complete Y")
- Segment-specific copy ("As a SaaS founder, you already know...")
- Dynamic content blocks based on purchase history or engagement tier

<Callout type="example">
**Duolingo (2024 streak recovery emails):** Duolingo's most clicked emails used zero generic copy. Each email referenced the exact streak length lost, the specific language being learned, and the user's previous session. CTR on personalized streak-recovery emails was 3.4x higher than their generic re-engagement template. The copy itself was minimal, the data did the persuading.
</Callout>

## CTAs: One Action, Maximum Clarity

Moosend's benchmark data is unambiguous: emails with a single CTA get 371% more clicks than emails with multiple CTAs. Every additional option you add reduces the probability that any action gets taken.

### CTA copy principles

**Weak CTA:** "Click here" / "Learn more" / "Submit"

**Strong CTA:** Completes the sentence "I want to ___"
- "Start my free trial"
- "Get the template"
- "See my personalized report"
- "Book a 15-minute call"

### Button vs. text link

Campaign Monitor found button CTAs outperform text links by 127%. Use a button for your primary CTA. If you must include a secondary link, make it plain text and place it lower in the email.

<Callout type="tip">
Add a P.S. line at the bottom of every email. Skimmers jump straight from the subject line to the P.S. Make it a one-line restatement of your CTA or your single most compelling benefit. It is the second most-read part of any email after the subject line.
</Callout>

## Real Company Examples

### Airbnb: The referral email that drove 300% more bookings

Airbnb's host referral email used three copywriting techniques simultaneously. The subject line led with a specific dollar amount ("Earn $75 for every host you refer"). The body copy used social proof ("Hosts like you have already earned over $2M"). The CTA was single, specific, and benefit-forward ("Send my referral link"). No fluff, no secondary offers, no company history. One goal, one path.

### The elements these examples share

All three examples (Grammarly, Duolingo, Airbnb) follow the same pattern:

1. Subject line promises a specific, credible outcome
2. Opening line starts with the reader's situation, not the sender's
3. Body copy is short, concrete, and uses real numbers
4. Single CTA with action-oriented copy
5. No decorative language that adds length without adding value

## Six Common Email Copy Mistakes

**1. Writing about features instead of outcomes**
"Our new dashboard has 47 data points" versus "See exactly which campaigns are bleeding budget in under 60 seconds."

**2. Burying the CTA**
If a reader has to scroll more than two screens to find your CTA, you have lost most of them. Put a CTA above the fold for emails longer than 300 words.

**3. Multiple competing goals**
One email for the sale, one email for the survey, one email for the webinar invite. Never combine them. Each goal dilutes the other.

**4. Passive, hedging language**
"We think you might be interested in possibly checking out..." Every hedge weakens conviction. State benefits directly.

**5. Ignoring mobile formatting**
Over 60% of emails are opened on mobile. Subject lines get cut at 33-40 characters on most phones. Single-column layouts and large tap targets are not optional.

**6. No preheader strategy**
Leaving preview text blank means email clients pull random body text, often a legal footer or an unsubscribe link. Write your preview text intentionally every single time.

## Key Takeaways

- Subject lines of 41-50 characters and question formats consistently outperform alternatives in 2025 data
- Open with the reader's problem, not your product's features
- PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution) remains the most reliable body copy structure for emails with a conversion goal
- One email = one CTA; every additional link cuts your click rate
- Personalization beyond first names (behavior, history, segment) multiplies engagement at every stage
- The P.S. line is not optional; it is the second most-read element in any email
