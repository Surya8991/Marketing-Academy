# StoryBrand Framework

## What It Is

The StoryBrand Framework (also called SB7) is a messaging system created by Donald Miller and published in his 2017 book "Building a StoryBrand." It gives businesses a repeatable structure for writing website copy, emails, and ads - one that humans are wired to respond to because it mirrors how stories work.

The core idea is simple but easy to get wrong: your customer is the hero of the story, not your brand. Most companies talk endlessly about themselves - their history, their awards, their mission. Customers do not care. They care about their own problem and whether you can solve it. StoryBrand forces you to flip the script.

The framework has 7 parts, often called the SB7. Each part maps to a classic story element. When you line them up in order and use them in your marketing, your message becomes dramatically clearer - and clearer messages convert better.

## Real-World Example

PunchLove Women's Fitness, an Australian gym, applied the StoryBrand framework to rebuild their marketing funnel in 2022. Before StoryBrand, their website talked about the gym's equipment and trainers. After the rewrite, every page put the woman (the customer) at the center - her problem, her goal, her transformation. They added a StoryBranded landing page, an email nurture sequence, and a Facebook Messenger bot. The result: an 800% increase in lead generation, tracked by Results and Co, a StoryBrand-certified agency.

## Why It Matters

Most businesses have a clarity problem, not a product problem. Their offer is good, but their website reads like a brochure about the company. Visitors land on the page, fail to see themselves in the message, and leave.

StoryBrand solves this by giving you a fill-in-the-blank script for every piece of marketing copy. It is especially useful when:

- You are rewriting a homepage that is not converting
- Your sales team cannot explain what you do in one clear sentence
- You are launching a new product and need a pitch
- Your ads get clicks but no conversions

According to StoryBrand's own data, over 100,000 businesses have gone through the framework since 2017. In December 2024, Donald Miller released StoryBrand 2.0, which added an AI writing tool that generates SB7-compliant copy for emails, landing pages, and social posts.

## How It Works

The 7 parts of SB7 follow the structure of a classic hero's journey - the same pattern found in Star Wars, The Lion King, and every story humans have told for thousands of years.

| Part | Story Element | What You Write |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | A Character | Your customer and what they want |
| 2 | Has a Problem | The external + internal problem they face |
| 3 | Meets a Guide | Your brand, positioned as the expert helper |
| 4 | Who Gives a Plan | Your 3-step process |
| 5 | And Calls to Action | Your primary CTA (buy) and secondary CTA (learn more) |
| 6 | That Ends in Success | The life the customer will have after buying |
| 7 | And Helps Them Avoid Failure | What they lose if they do not act |

The most important shift is Part 3: you are the guide, not the hero. Think Yoda (not Luke). Think Gandalf (not Frodo). Your brand's job is to make the customer look good, not the other way around.

### Writing the BrandScript

Before touching your website, StoryBrand asks you to fill out a one-page document called the BrandScript. It has one field for each of the 7 parts. Once it is complete, every headline, email subject line, and ad you write should pull directly from it. The BrandScript keeps your whole team aligned on the same message.

## Common Mistakes

<Callout type="warning">
The single biggest mistake is making your brand the hero. It shows up in headlines like "We are the industry leader in..." or "Our award-winning team delivers..." - both of which put the spotlight on you instead of the customer. The fix: rewrite every headline so it describes what the customer wants or what problem they have. Your brand should only appear as the trusted guide who helps them get there.
</Callout>

A close second mistake is skipping Part 7 (failure). Many brands feel uncomfortable mentioning what customers lose by not buying. But humans are wired to avoid loss more than they seek gain - a principle called loss aversion. Naming the cost of inaction in your copy ("Without a clear plan, you risk losing customers to competitors who communicate better") almost always increases conversion.

## The One-Line Takeaway

**If your customer cannot immediately see themselves in your marketing, they will not buy - StoryBrand gives you the exact script to make them the hero of every message you write.**
