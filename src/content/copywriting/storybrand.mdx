# StoryBrand Framework

In 2025, the average B2B website converts at just 1.8% of visitors to leads. The StoryBrand framework is the most battle-tested system for fixing that number by doing one deceptively simple thing: making your customer the hero and your brand the guide.

## Quick Summary

- StoryBrand (SB7) is a 7-part messaging framework built on classic story structure, published by Donald Miller in 2017 and fully revised as StoryBrand 2.0 in January 2025.
- The core shift: stop talking about your brand, start talking about your customer's problem and transformation.
- The framework produces a one-page document called the BrandScript that drives every headline, email, ad, and sales pitch your team writes.
- Over 1 million business leaders have used the framework since 2017, according to HarperCollins Leadership.
- Real-world results include an 800% lead increase (PunchLove Women's Fitness), a 28% lift in demo requests, and a 44% boost in lead-to-opportunity conversions for a B2B SaaS company, all within 90 days.

## What It Actually Is

StoryBrand is a fill-in-the-blank messaging script based on the structure of every story humans have ever told. A character wants something, faces a problem, meets a guide, gets a plan, takes action, and either wins or loses.

The analogy that clicks for most marketers: think of your brand as a GPS, not a tour guide showing off its own knowledge. The GPS does not talk about how it was built or how many awards it has won. It says "turn left in 200 metres" and gets the driver where they need to go. StoryBrand turns your marketing into that GPS.

## How It Works: The 7-Part BrandScript

Before writing a single word of copy, StoryBrand asks you to complete a one-page document called the BrandScript. It has exactly one field per framework element. Everything your team writes, homepage headline, sales email, ad copy, pitch deck, should pull directly from this document.

Here are the 7 parts and what you write for each:

### Part 1: A Character (your customer's desire)

Name the one thing your ideal customer wants most. Not a list of features. One desire. Example: "I want to stop losing deals because my pitch is confusing."

### Part 2: Has a Problem (three levels)

StoryBrand splits the problem into three layers:

- **External:** The surface-level, observable problem. "My website does not generate leads."
- **Internal:** The emotional frustration underneath it. "I feel embarrassed that my competitors look more professional than me."
- **Philosophical:** The deeper injustice. "It is wrong that a great product should fail because of bad messaging."

Most brands only address the external problem. Naming the internal problem is what creates emotional resonance.

### Part 3: Meets a Guide (your brand)

This is where your brand enters, but only as a supporting character. The guide shows two qualities:

1. **Empathy:** "We understand how frustrating it is to have a great product that people do not understand."
2. **Authority:** Proof that you can help. Testimonials, client logos, certifications, specific numbers.

Think Yoda, not Luke. Think Gandalf, not Frodo. The guide's job is to make the hero look good.

### Part 4: Who Gives a Plan (your process)

Give the customer a clear 3-step process. The number three is not arbitrary, research on decision fatigue shows more steps create hesitation. Example:

1. Book a free 30-minute messaging audit.
2. We rewrite your homepage headline and BrandScript.
3. You launch copy that converts.

### Part 5: And Calls Them to Action (CTA)

Two CTAs, always:

- **Direct CTA:** "Buy now," "Book a call," "Get started." This is the primary ask.
- **Transitional CTA:** "Download the free guide," "Watch the demo." This is for visitors who are not ready to buy yet.

Most brands have only one CTA and lose the 70-80% of visitors who are in research mode.

### Part 6: That Ends in Success (the transformation)

Paint a vivid picture of the customer's life after they buy. Be specific. "You will have a homepage that explains what you do in 5 seconds or less" beats "You will have better marketing." Specificity makes the transformation feel real and attainable.

### Part 7: And Helps Them Avoid Failure (the stakes)

Name what the customer loses if they do not act. This feels uncomfortable to many brands, but loss aversion is one of the most robust findings in behavioural psychology: humans feel losses roughly twice as intensely as equivalent gains. Naming the cost of inaction ("Every week without a clear message is another week your competitors are winning the customers who should be yours") almost always increases conversion.

### Filling Out the BrandScript

The BrandScript is the output of the process above, compressed onto one page. Once it is done, every person on your team, sales, marketing, customer support, is reading from the same script. The BrandScript also feeds directly into StoryBrand AI (launched January 2025) to auto-generate compliant copy across channels.

The official free BrandScript builder is at [mystorybrand.com](https://www.mystorybrand.com). Fill it out before you touch your website.

## Real Company Examples

### PunchLove Women's Fitness (Australia, 2022)

PunchLove is a women-only gym in Brisbane. Before StoryBrand, their homepage headline was "Best Women's Gym in Brisbane", a brand-centric claim that said nothing about the customer. After a full SB7 rewrite led by Results and Co (a StoryBrand-certified agency), the headline became "Get Fit Without Giving Up Your Life." Every page was rewritten to put the woman's transformation at the centre. They added a StoryBranded landing page, an email nurture sequence, and a Facebook Messenger bot. The result: [an 800% increase in lead generation](https://resultsandco.com.au/blog/storybrand-case-study-results), tracked by Results and Co.

<Callout type="example">
PunchLove's headline change illustrates the entire framework in one line. "Best Women's Gym in Brisbane" makes the gym the hero. "Get Fit Without Giving Up Your Life" makes the customer the hero. Same product, same price, same team, completely different conversion rate. The 800% lead increase came from clarity, not ad spend.
</Callout>

### B2B SaaS Company (Case Study, 2024)

A mid-sized B2B SaaS company restructured its website messaging using the SB7 framework in 2024. The changes were confined to copy and page architecture, no new design, no new traffic. According to [LeadGen Economy's analysis](https://www.leadgen-economy.com/blog/storybrand-framework-b2b-lead-gen-ecommerce-funnels/), within 90 days the company saw:

- 28% increase in demo requests
- 44% boost in lead-to-opportunity conversions

The critical change: the homepage stopped leading with feature lists and started leading with the customer's problem statement. The old headline described the software. The new headline described the frustration the software solves.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Making your brand the hero

The most common and most damaging mistake. It shows up as: "We are the industry leader in...", "Our award-winning team delivers...", "Since 1987, we have been committed to..." Every one of these sentences puts the spotlight on the brand. The customer is not looking for a hero to admire, they are looking for a guide to help them succeed. Rewrite every headline so it names what the customer wants or what problem they face.

### Mistake 2: Skipping the internal problem

Most brands only describe the external problem (the observable situation). The internal problem is the emotional frustration underneath it, and that is what actually drives purchase decisions. A cybersecurity company's external problem is "your data is at risk." The internal problem is "I feel like I am one breach away from destroying everything I built." Copy that names the internal problem creates immediate recognition and trust.

### Mistake 3: Having only one CTA

Only 20-30% of your visitors are ready to buy on the first visit. If your only CTA is "Buy now," you are ignoring the majority of your traffic. StoryBrand explicitly requires a transitional CTA, a lower-commitment option (free guide, webinar, demo video) that keeps non-buyers in your orbit until they are ready. Single-goal landing pages outperform multi-CTA pages on conversion, but you need two separate pages for two separate audiences.

### Mistake 4: Skipping Part 7 (failure)

Loss aversion is not optional in human psychology, it is hardwired. Skipping the failure section because it feels negative leaves the most powerful motivator unused. Name what the customer risks by doing nothing. Keep it factual and relevant, not fear-mongering. "Without a clear message, your best customers will choose a competitor who explains their offer more simply" is specific and credible.

### Mistake 5: Writing the BrandScript once and filing it away

The BrandScript is a living document, not a one-time exercise. Your customer's language changes. Your offer evolves. Your market shifts. StoryBrand-certified agencies recommend revisiting the BrandScript every 12 months and any time you launch a new product line, enter a new market, or see a significant drop in conversion rates.

## Key Takeaways

- Your customer is the hero. Your brand is the guide. This one shift is the entire framework.
- The internal problem (emotional frustration) converts better than the external problem (observable situation), name both.
- The BrandScript is the foundation: complete it before writing a single word of copy.
- Always include a transitional CTA alongside your primary CTA to capture visitors who are not ready to buy.
- Loss aversion is real: naming the cost of inaction in Part 7 consistently increases conversion.
- StoryBrand 2.0 (January 2025) added AI-powered copy generation, if you are applying the framework manually, you are working harder than you need to.
