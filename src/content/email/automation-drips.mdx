# Automation and Drip Flows

If you only send broadcast campaigns, you are leaving most of your email revenue on the table. Automated flows fire on customer behavior, run while you sleep, and out-earn campaigns by a wide margin. This lesson is for marketers and founders who already collect emails but have not yet built the always-on machine behind them.

## What It Actually Is

A drip flow is a sequence of emails (and sometimes SMS) triggered by a specific event: someone joins a list, abandons a cart, browses a category three times, hits a 60-day silence, or buys for the second time. Each email is pre-written, the timing is pre-set, and the system sends it to the right person automatically.

Example: a shopper adds a hoodie to cart and leaves. One hour later they get an email with the hoodie image and a 'Still thinking?' subject line. Twenty-four hours later, a second email shows reviews. Forty-six hours after that, a 10% code. They never knew you scheduled it months ago.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The numbers are lopsided. According to [Klaviyo's 2025 benchmark report](https://www.klaviyo.com/products/email-marketing/benchmarks), flows generate roughly **41% of total email revenue from just 5.3% of sends**, with revenue per recipient nearly **18x higher than one-off campaigns**. Abandoned cart flows alone average **$3.65 RPR vs $0.11** for standard campaigns.

The single most important flow is the welcome series. [Stripo's 2024 data](https://stripo.email/blog/abandoned-cart-email-statistics-insights-and-key-metrics-for-boosting-conversions/) shows welcome emails generate **320% more revenue than promotional emails** and routinely hit 50%+ open rates because the subscriber just raised their hand.

Timing compounds the effect. [Klaviyo's abandoned cart benchmark](https://www.klaviyo.com/blog/abandoned-cart-benchmarks) shows recovery emails sent within one hour convert at **20.3%**, while those sent after 24 hours drop to **12.2%**. The top 10% of brands run at least **10 active flows** and pull RPR up to **$7.79**.

## How It Works / The Playbook

Build these seven flows in order. Do not skip ahead — each one feeds the next.

1. **Welcome series (3-5 emails).** Trigger: list signup. Email 1 immediate (deliver the lead magnet or discount). Email 2 at +1 day (brand story). Email 3 at +3 days (best sellers or social proof). Email 4 at +5 days (objection handler or urgency).
2. **Abandoned cart (3 emails).** Trigger: checkout started, not completed. Send at +1 hour, +24 hours, +48 hours. Discount only on email 3.
3. **Browse abandonment (2 emails).** Trigger: viewed product, did not add to cart. Send at +4 hours and +24 hours. Lower urgency than cart.
4. **Post-purchase (3-4 emails).** Trigger: order placed. Order confirmation, shipping notification, 'how to use it' guide at +7 days, review request at +14-21 days.
5. **Win-back (2-3 emails).** Trigger: no purchase in 60-90 days. Lead with 'we miss you' and a meaningful incentive.
6. **Replenishment.** Trigger: time since last purchase based on consumable cycle (e.g., 30 days for coffee, 90 days for vitamins).
7. **VIP / second-purchase flow.** Trigger: second order placed. Treat them differently because they are a different customer.

For each flow: write the copy first, then split-test subject lines, then layer in dynamic product blocks. Do not start with personalization tokens — start with one good email.

## Common Mistakes

- **Building one giant flow instead of many small ones.** A 12-email welcome cannot also be the cart recovery. One trigger, one job.
- **Running every flow at full discount.** If new subscribers, abandoners, and lapsed buyers all get 20% off, you have trained everyone to wait. Reserve discounts for email 2 or 3.
- **Ignoring flow exits.** A subscriber who buys should drop out of the welcome series immediately. Otherwise you are pitching the product they just bought.
- **No suppression for repeat triggers.** Browsing 14 products generates 14 'Still interested?' emails unless you cap or de-duplicate.
- **Set and forget.** Flows decay. Revisit copy, offers, and timing every quarter. The top-performer benchmark moves every year.

## Key Takeaways

- Flows produce around 41% of email revenue from 5% of sends. Build them before you send another newsletter.
- Welcome, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase, win-back, replenishment, and VIP are the seven flows that cover 90% of revenue lift.
- Speed wins on cart recovery: first email within one hour or you halve the conversion rate.
