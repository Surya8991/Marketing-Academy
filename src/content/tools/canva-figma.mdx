# Canva vs Figma for Marketers

Every marketer needs to produce visuals. Social posts, ads, email headers, landing page mockups, the list never ends. Two tools dominate: Canva and Figma. They look similar at first glance (both run in the browser, both let you drag and drop shapes), but they solve completely different problems. Picking the wrong one for a task costs you hours every week.

## Quick Summary

- Canva is built for speed: non-designers can ship a social post in under 5 minutes using templates.
- Figma is built for precision: designers and developers use it to build and hand off production-ready web assets.
- By end of 2025, Canva had 265 million monthly active users and $4 billion in annual recurring revenue.
- Salesforce used Canva Enterprise and cut cost-per-design by 66% while producing 63% more assets.
- Most marketing teams run both: Canva for daily content, Figma for anything touching engineering.

## What Each Tool Actually Does

### Canva, The Marketer's Workhorse

Canva is a template-first design tool. You pick a size (Instagram post, LinkedIn banner, presentation slide), browse templates, swap in your text and images, apply your brand colors, and export. Done.

Key features for marketers:
- **Brand Kit**: upload your logo, fonts, and color hex codes once. Every new design pulls them in automatically.
- **Magic Resize**: take one Instagram post and resize it to a Facebook ad, LinkedIn banner, and Twitter card in one click.
- **Magic Write**: AI copywriting built into the editor. Generate captions, headlines, or slide bullets without leaving the canvas.
- **Magic Studio**: the umbrella AI suite covering image generation, background removal, and translation.
- Over 3.6 million templates on paid plans, 1.6 million on the free tier. 141 million stock photos, graphics, and videos.

**Best for:** social media graphics, email headers, pitch decks, one-pagers, blog thumbnails, short-form video, print flyers.

### Figma, The Designer-Developer Bridge

Figma is a vector design tool with a heavy focus on components (reusable design pieces), prototyping (clickable mockups), and developer handoff (sharing design specs with engineers). It has about 1,800 plugins and integrates with tools like Webflow and Shopify.

Key features for marketers who collaborate with developers:
- **Components**: design a button once, use it everywhere. Change it once and it updates in every file.
- **Prototypes**: link frames together to build a clickable walkthrough of a landing page or app flow. Show stakeholders how a page actually behaves before a developer writes one line of code.
- **Dev Mode**: developers open the same file and see exact pixel measurements, CSS values, and exportable assets. Zero guesswork, fewer revision rounds.
- **Figma Buzz (2025)**: a new product inside Figma designed for marketing teams. Lets marketers create ads, social posts, and banners using a company's existing Figma design system, keeping brand consistency with the product team.

**Best for:** landing page mockups, web banner sets, ad creative requiring dev handoff, UI copy reviews, design system management.

The smartest marketing teams do not choose one or the other. They use both with clear rules about which job goes where.

## Setting Up Your Brand Kit (Canva)

A Brand Kit is the single most valuable 15-minute investment you can make in Canva. Once set up, every design pulls your brand identity in automatically.

1. Go to Brand Hub in Canva (top nav, gear icon).
2. Upload your logo in PNG with transparent background.
3. Add your primary, secondary, and accent hex color codes.
4. Upload your brand fonts (or select the closest match from Canva's library).
5. Set a brand voice for Magic Write (formal, casual, bold, etc.).

From that point on, when you open any template, Canva surfaces your brand colors and fonts first. You stop spending five minutes hunting for your hex code on every new asset.

<Callout type="example">
A two-person marketing team at a Series A SaaS startup set up their Canva Brand Kit on day one with three colors, two fonts, and a logo. Six months later they had produced over 400 social assets, two investor decks, and a full email sequence, all on-brand, zero design agency invoices. The Brand Kit was the only reason this was possible without a full-time designer.
</Callout>

## Setting Up a Figma Component Library (For Teams)

If your company has a Figma account, a shared component library removes the "what button style do we use?" question forever.

1. Create a new Figma file named "Brand Design System."
2. Build your core components: primary button, secondary button, heading styles, card layout, navigation bar.
3. Publish the library (Assets panel > Libraries > Publish).
4. Every team member's Figma file can now use these components via the Assets panel.
5. When a brand color changes, update it in the library and every file updates automatically.

This matters for marketers because it means the landing page mockup you send to engineering already uses the exact same button, font size, and spacing that is live in production. Revision rounds drop significantly.

## AI Features Worth Knowing in 2025

Both tools have gone heavy on AI. Here is what is actually useful:

**Canva Magic Studio:**
- Magic Eraser, remove objects from photos without Photoshop.
- Magic Expand, extend an image beyond its original borders (useful for repurposing portrait images for landscape formats).
- Magic Write, generates copy inside the canvas. Works best with a short prompt like "5 LinkedIn post hooks for a B2B SaaS company launching a new reporting feature."
- Magic Animate, adds entrance animations to presentation slides in one click.

**Figma AI (2025):**
- Figma Make, converts a text prompt into a working prototype. Type "a pricing page with three tiers and a toggle for annual/monthly" and Figma builds a rough version.
- AI search across components, find the right component by describing it instead of scrolling through a list.
- Auto layout suggestions, Figma now suggests responsive layout rules as you design.

<Callout type="warning">
AI-generated designs are starting points, not finished assets. Magic Write copy needs a human edit for tone and accuracy. Figma Make prototypes have structural flaws that a developer will flag immediately. Use AI to reduce blank-page paralysis and rough out ideas, then refine before publishing or sharing.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes Marketers Make

1. **Using Figma for social posts.** Figma has no template library for Instagram or LinkedIn formats. You will spend 20 minutes setting up a canvas that Canva has ready in 10 seconds.

2. **Using Canva for developer handoff.** Canva does not output CSS values or component specs. If you hand a Canva export to a developer, they will rebuild the design from scratch. Use Figma.

3. **Skipping the Brand Kit entirely.** Without a Brand Kit, every designer and marketer picks slightly different shades of blue. Brand drift is invisible until you print a brochure and it does not match your website.

4. **Maintaining separate brand kits in both tools.** If your hex codes change, you must update both. Designate one source of truth (Figma for product design, Canva for marketing) and make updates in both on the same day.

5. **Paying for Canva Pro without using Brand Kit, Magic Resize, or background removal.** If you are only using free templates, you are on the wrong plan.

- Free tier: 3 files, 3 projects, unlimited collaborators (view only).

For most early-stage marketing teams: Canva Pro is the first paid tool to buy. Figma Professional comes next when you have a developer to hand off to.

## The Workflow in Practice: A Realistic Week

Here is how a growth marketer at a mid-stage startup might use both tools in one week:

- **Monday:** New blog post goes live. Open Canva, pick a blog thumbnail template, apply Brand Kit, write headline with Magic Write, export in 8 minutes.

Two tools, one week, zero design agency tickets.
