# All-in-One Marketing Platforms

Every growth team eventually hits the wall: ten point tools, three CRMs of record, attribution that nobody trusts, and a quarterly bill nobody wants to open. That is when leadership starts asking about a suite. This lesson is for the operator deciding whether to consolidate onto HubSpot, Salesforce Marketing Cloud, or Adobe Marketo Engage, or whether to stay best-of-breed.

## What It Actually Is

An all-in-one marketing platform bundles email, landing pages, forms, CRM sync, lead scoring, journey orchestration, analytics, and increasingly AI agents into a single billing relationship and (in theory) a single data model. A concrete example: instead of running Mailchimp + Unbounce + Segment + a separate lead-scoring tool plus a Zapier mesh holding it together, a team standardizes on HubSpot Marketing Hub Enterprise so every form fill, email open, and deal stage lives in one contact record.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The market has consolidated hard around three vendors, but not evenly. As of 2025, HubSpot holds roughly 29.36% of marketing automation market share, dwarfing Adobe Marketo Engage at around 5.46%, per industry tracking summarized by [Monetizely](https://www.getmonetizely.com/articles/hubspot-vs-salesforce-vs-marketo-which-marketing-automation-platform-offers-the-best-value). HubSpot was also named a Leader in [Gartner's 2025 Magic Quadrant for B2B Marketing Automation Platforms](https://lovable.dev/guides/hubspot-vs-marketo), alongside Adobe and Salesforce.

The pricing gap explains a lot of the share shift. Per [etropo's 2025 pricing comparison](https://www.etropo.com/marketing-tool-prices/marketing-automation), HubSpot Marketing Hub Professional runs $890/month for 2,000 contacts with a mandatory $3,000 onboarding fee, while Enterprise is $3,600/month plus a $7,000 onboarding fee. Salesforce Marketing Cloud Engagement starts around $1,250/month and scales steeply once Data Cloud, Personalization, and Account Engagement (formerly Pardot) are layered in. Marketo Engage typically lands between the two but quotes are bespoke.

Ease of setup is the other tell. G2 users rate HubSpot's setup at 8.1/10 vs Marketo's 6.7/10, a gap that compounds across multi-quarter migrations.

## How It Works / The Playbook

Pick the suite that matches your data gravity and team maturity, not the one with the loudest sales rep.

1. **Map the data of record.** If your single source of truth is Salesforce CRM, Marketing Cloud Engagement + Data Cloud will integrate natively. If you have no CRM or a fragmented one, HubSpot's Smart CRM removes a migration project.
2. **Score your operational maturity.** Marketo rewards teams with a dedicated marketing ops function: tokens, smart campaigns, and Revenue Cycle Analytics are powerful but unforgiving. HubSpot rewards generalists.
3. **Pressure-test the journey complexity.** Multi-brand, multi-region, multi-language journeys with 1:1 personalization on millions of records is the Marketing Cloud sweet spot. Single-funnel B2B SaaS rarely needs that.
4. **Model 3-year TCO, not Year 1.** Add seats, contact tier overages, sandbox costs, implementation partner fees (usually 1-2x the platform cost in Year 1), and the dedicated admin headcount.
5. **Audit the AI roadmap.** HubSpot's Breeze agents, Salesforce's Agentforce, and Adobe's AI Assistant in Marketo all shipped meaningful capabilities in 2025. Ask vendors which agents are GA vs pilot.
6. **Negotiate the exit.** Insist on data export rights and journey export formats in the MSA. Suites are sticky on purpose.

<Callout type="example">
A mid-market B2B SaaS running Marketo Engage with Salesforce CRM typically spends $40,000-$80,000/year on Marketo plus a $60,000-$120,000 implementation partner engagement in Year 1, per the [Digital Applied 2026 comparison guide](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/marketing-automation-platform-comparison-2026). The same company on HubSpot Marketing Hub Enterprise at $3,600/month lands at roughly $43,200/year in license fees plus a $7,000 onboarding, a Year 1 swing of well over $100,000, which is why so many sub-1,000-employee B2B teams have migrated off Marketo since 2023.

## Common Mistakes

- Buying Enterprise tiers for features you will not configure in the first 12 months. Most teams use under 40% of the Professional tier feature set.
- Treating the suite as a CRM replacement when sales already lives in Salesforce. You will end up with two systems of record and zero trust in either.
- Skipping the data model design. Custom objects, lifecycle stages, and lead-to-account matching should be designed before a single workflow is built.
- Letting the implementation partner own the build. Knowledge transfer rarely happens and you inherit a black box.
- Underestimating the headcount. Marketo Engage realistically needs at least one full-time marketing ops admin; Marketing Cloud often needs two plus a developer.

## Key Takeaways

- Suite vs best-of-breed is a question of data gravity and ops maturity, not feature checklists.
- HubSpot wins on time-to-value and SMB/mid-market economics; Salesforce Marketing Cloud wins on enterprise journey complexity; Marketo wins on B2B demand gen depth when you have the ops team to run it.
- Year 1 license cost is rarely the real cost: implementation, headcount, and contact-tier overages dominate 3-year TCO.
