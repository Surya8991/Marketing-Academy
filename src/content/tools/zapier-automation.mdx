## What Is Zapier, Actually?

Think of Zapier as a universal translator sitting between your apps.

Without Zapier, each tool you use is an island. A lead fills your Typeform. You manually copy their details into your CRM. You remember (sometimes) to add them to your email list. You try to ping your sales team on Slack. Each step is manual, slow, and prone to being skipped.

With Zapier, the moment someone fills that Typeform, all of those things happen automatically in under 10 seconds, while you are asleep, in a meeting, or doing something that actually requires your brain.

The vocabulary you need to know:

- **Zap**: one complete automation. "When X happens in App A, do Y in App B."
- **Trigger**: the event that starts the Zap (new form submission, new email, new payment, new row in a spreadsheet)
- **Action**: what Zapier does after the trigger fires (create a contact, send a message, update a spreadsheet)
- **Task**: one execution of one action step. Zapier's pricing is based on how many tasks you use per month.
- **Multi-step Zap**: a Zap with one trigger and multiple actions (create CRM contact AND send Slack alert AND add to email list)
- **Filter**: a condition that controls when a Zap runs (only trigger if the country is "United States," only if deal value exceeds $500)
- **Paths**: branching logic, like an if/else. Send high-value leads one way, low-value leads another way.

<Callout type="info">
Zapier's pricing is based on "tasks." Each time an action step runs, it counts as one task. A 3-action Zap that runs 100 times per month uses 300 tasks. The free plan gives 100 tasks per month and 5 Zaps. Starter ($20/month) gives 750 tasks. Most small marketing teams land on Professional ($49/month) for 2,000 tasks, unlimited Zaps, and filters. Enterprise teams average $48,000 per year according to Zapier's 2026 revenue breakdown.

## The 10 Most Valuable Marketing Zaps

These automations save the most time for the most marketers. Start with whichever matches your biggest pain point.

### 1. Lead Capture to CRM

**Trigger:** New form submission (Typeform, Gravity Forms, Facebook Lead Ads, LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms)
**Action:** Create or update contact in CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive)

This is the most common Zap in marketing. Research consistently shows that responding to a lead within 5 minutes increases conversion probability by 9x compared to waiting 30 minutes. Manual copy-paste is the enemy of speed.

### 2. Lead Notification to Sales Team

**Trigger:** New contact created in CRM
**Action:** Send Slack message to #sales-leads channel with name, company, source, and lead score

Context matters in the notification. Include which form the lead filled, which campaign drove them, and which page they came from. That context tells a salesperson whether to call in 5 minutes or tomorrow morning.

### 3. Email List Sync

**Trigger:** New contact created or tagged in CRM
**Action:** Add subscriber to the correct Mailchimp or Klaviyo audience with the correct tags

This eliminates the export-import cycle that causes list drift, the problem where your CRM and email tool have different data for the same person.

### 4. Blog Post to Social Publishing

**Trigger:** New post published in WordPress (or new row in Airtable content calendar)
**Action:** Post to LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and Facebook automatically

This saves roughly an hour per content piece for most marketing teams. The content goes out the moment it publishes, not whenever someone remembers.

### 5. Webinar Registration to Nurture Sequence

**Trigger:** New Zoom or Eventbrite registrant
**Action:** Add to HubSpot with tag "webinar-registered" and enroll in a pre-event email sequence

No more manual Zoom-to-email-list migration before each event. The moment someone registers, the nurture sequence starts.

### 6. E-commerce Purchase to Email Segmentation

**Trigger:** New completed order in Shopify or WooCommerce
**Action:** Tag customer in Klaviyo by product category purchased and trigger a post-purchase flow

This is how e-commerce brands build "purchased X but not Y" segments automatically, enabling cross-sell campaigns without anyone touching a spreadsheet.

### 7. Survey Response to CRM Note

**Trigger:** New Typeform NPS or feedback response
**Action:** Find the matching CRM contact and add the score and response text as a note on their record

Feedback stops living in survey tool silos and becomes part of the contact record where sales and support can actually see it.

### 8. Hot Lead to Retargeting Audience

**Trigger:** Contact tagged "hot" or "SQL" (sales-qualified lead) in CRM
**Action:** Add to Facebook Custom Audience for retargeting ads

Instead of manually uploading CSV lists to Facebook Ads Manager every week, your hottest leads are automatically added to a retargeting audience the moment they hit the right score.

### 9. Google Ads Lead Form to CRM

**Trigger:** New lead from Google Ads Lead Form Extension
**Action:** Create HubSpot contact and trigger an email follow-up sequence

Google's native Lead Form ads have no automatic CRM integration. Leads sit in the Google Ads interface until someone downloads them. Zapier fixes this immediately.

### 10. Payment Received to Deal Closed

**Trigger:** Invoice paid in Stripe or QuickBooks
**Action:** Move deal to "Closed Won" in CRM, send a thank-you email, and create an onboarding task for the success team

The sales cycle close is usually logged manually and often delayed. This closes the loop automatically the moment payment hits.

## How to Build Your First Zap in 20 Minutes

**Step 1, Identify your pain point.** What do you do manually more than 5 times per week? Write it as a sentence: "When [event in App A] happens, I always have to [action in App B]."

**Step 2, Go to zapier.com, sign up for free, and click "Create Zap."**

**Step 3, Set your trigger.** Search for your trigger app (example: "Typeform"). Choose the event type (example: "New Entry"). Connect your account using OAuth, you just click "Allow." Select the specific form. Click "Test Trigger", Zapier pulls in a recent submission so you can see the exact data fields available.

**Step 4, Set your action.** Search for your destination app (example: "HubSpot"). Choose the event type (example: "Create Contact"). Connect your account. Map the fields: drag Typeform's "Email" field to HubSpot's "Email" field. Click "Test Action" to create a test contact.

**Step 5, Name your Zap and turn it on.** It runs automatically from this point forward. You will see every run logged in the Zap's history.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<Callout type="warning">
**Mistake 1, Skipping the test.** Zapier has a built-in test feature for both triggers and actions. Always run a full test with real sample data before turning a Zap on. Blank fields, unexpected data formats, and app permission issues only reveal themselves during testing.

**Mistake 2, Building before mapping.** Open a blank doc and write out the full logic first: "When X, AND if Y, then do A then B then C." Marketers who jump straight into the Zapier interface often discover broken logic three steps into a 5-action Zap.

**Mistake 3, Ignoring task volume.** A single high-traffic lead form can burn through a free plan in days. Estimate before you build: if you get 400 form submissions per month and your Zap has 3 action steps, that is 1,200 tasks per month. Plan your tier before you launch.

**Mistake 4, No error notifications.** Zaps can fail silently if an app connection expires or an API limit is hit. Set up email notifications for Zap errors in your Zapier account settings so failures do not go undetected for days.
</Callout>

## Zapier vs. Make vs. n8n: Which One to Use

Zapier is not the only automation tool. Here is the honest comparison:

| Tool | Best For | Pricing Model | Technical Skill Needed |
|------|----------|---------------|------------------------|
| Zapier | Non-technical marketers, fastest setup, 8,500+ apps | Per task | None |
| Make (formerly Integromat) | Complex branching logic, more affordable for high volume | Per operation (cheaper) | Low |
| n8n | AI agent workflows, self-hosting, unlimited runs | Per execution or self-hosted free | Moderate |

**Use Zapier if:** You are a marketer without a developer, you want to launch in an afternoon, and you need reliable connectors for mainstream apps (HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp, Shopify, Slack, Google Workspace).

**Switch to Make if:** Your Zaps have become complex 10-step workflows with lots of branching, and Zapier's task costs are becoming significant.

**Consider n8n if:** Your team has a developer who can manage a hosted instance, and you need to chain LLM (AI model) calls into your automation workflows.
