# Top 10 Analytics Tools

Every marketer eventually hits the same wall: GA4 tells you traffic is up but not why a feature flopped, and a product analytics tool tells you about funnels but nothing about paid attribution. Picking the right tool for the right question saves weeks of dashboard-building and tens of thousands of dollars in licenses. This lesson maps the ten tools that matter in 2025 to the job each one was built for.

## What It Actually Is

A marketing analytics stack is a layered set of tools: collection (GA4, Segment), product behavior (Mixpanel, Amplitude), qualitative (Hotjar), and visualization (Looker Studio, Tableau). One concrete example: a SaaS team might pipe GA4 for acquisition channels, Mixpanel for in-app funnels, Hotjar for friction on the pricing page, and Looker Studio to roll all three into a single CMO dashboard.

## Why It Matters (with data)

Google Analytics still dominates raw install share at roughly 85.3% of sites that use any analytics, but spend on specialized tools (Amplitude, Mixpanel, PostHog, Plausible) is growing about 28% year over year. The total product analytics market is projected to reach $25.4 billion by 2026 at an 18.3% CAGR per [Monetizely's 2025 analysis](https://www.getmonetizely.com/articles/mixpanel-vs-amplitude-vs-google-analytics-which-product-analytics-tool-offers-best-value-for-saas-in-2025). The split matters: GA4 is session-based and tuned for marketing attribution, while Mixpanel and Amplitude are event-based and built for behavioral product questions, per [Amplitude's 2026 product analytics roundup](https://amplitude.com/compare/best-product-analytics-tools).

## How It Works / The Playbook

The ten tools worth knowing, grouped by job-to-be-done:

1. **Google Analytics 4 (GA4)** - free, session-and-event hybrid, best for channel attribution and SEO/paid traffic reporting. Default for almost every site.
2. **Mixpanel** - event-based product analytics. Free up to 20M events/month in 2025; Growth plan starts at $20/month. Best for funnels, cohorts, and retention on SaaS or apps.
3. **Amplitude** - event-based with strong experimentation and behavioral cohorts. Free Starter covers 50,000 MTUs and 10M events/month; Plus starts at $49/month; Growth/Enterprise commonly $30K to $100K+/year.
4. **Hotjar** - qualitative: heatmaps, session recordings, on-page surveys. Free tier captures up to 35 sessions/day. Use it to explain the why behind a GA4 drop.
5. **Looker Studio** (formerly Google Data Studio) - free drag-and-drop dashboards that connect to GA4, BigQuery, Sheets, and most ad platforms.
6. **Looker** (the enterprise BI platform inside Google Cloud) - LookML-based semantic modeling for company-wide data governance. Different product from Looker Studio.
7. **Adobe Analytics** - enterprise web/app analytics with deep segmentation, common in Fortune 500 marketing teams.
8. **Segment** - customer data platform (CDP) that collects events once and forwards them to GA4, Mixpanel, Amplitude, and your warehouse.
9. **PostHog** - open-source product analytics with feature flags, session replay, and A/B testing in one tool. Self-hostable.
10. **Tableau** - heavyweight BI for blended data sources, common when finance and marketing share dashboards.

Pick by question, not by brand:

- "Where is traffic coming from?" - GA4
- "Where are users dropping off in onboarding?" - Mixpanel or Amplitude
- "Why are they dropping off?" - Hotjar
- "Can a non-analyst build the weekly report?" - Looker Studio
- "Can we govern 200 dashboards across the company?" - Looker or Tableau

## Common Mistakes

- Using GA4 to answer product questions. Session-based sampling and 14-month data retention defaults make funnel and cohort analysis painful.
- Buying Amplitude or Mixpanel before you have a tagged event schema. Both tools are only as good as the events you send.
- Treating Hotjar as a metrics tool. It is qualitative, not statistical, and 35 sessions/day on the free tier is not a sample size for decisions.
- Confusing Looker Studio (free dashboards) with Looker (enterprise BI starting in the five figures annually).
- Skipping a CDP like Segment and instead instrumenting each tool separately, which guarantees three tools will disagree on the same number within a quarter.

## Key Takeaways

- GA4 owns acquisition, Mixpanel and Amplitude own behavior, Hotjar owns the why, Looker Studio owns the dashboard layer.
- Free tiers (GA4, Mixpanel 20M events, Amplitude 50K MTUs, Hotjar 35 sessions/day, Looker Studio, PostHog) cover most early-stage stacks at zero cost.
- Define the question first, then choose the tool. Buying a $60K Amplitude contract before you have an event schema is the most expensive mistake on this list.
