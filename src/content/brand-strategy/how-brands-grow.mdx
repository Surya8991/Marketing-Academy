## Why Most Loyalty Strategies Backfire

Most marketers are taught to love their loyal customers above all else. Reward programs, VIP tiers, re-engagement flows - the whole playbook assumes that deepening loyalty is the path to growth.

Byron Sharp's research at the Ehrenberg-Bass Institute flips that assumption.

**Real stat:** Coca-Cola's top 4% of buyers account for roughly 25% of its yearly sales volume (Ehrenberg-Bass Institute, 2023). That sounds impressive until you realise the other 75% of volume comes from infrequent, light buyers - the exact people most loyalty programs ignore.

<Callout type="warning">
Focusing your entire budget on your most loyal customers is a ceiling strategy, not a growth strategy. Loyal buyers are already buying. Growth requires reaching the people who rarely think of you.
</Callout>

## The Double Jeopardy Law

Every large brand shares two linked advantages over smaller rivals:

1. More buyers overall
2. Those buyers purchase slightly more often

This is called the Double Jeopardy Law. Smaller brands suffer on both counts: fewer buyers AND lower purchase frequency. The law holds across categories from soft drinks to banking to car insurance.

The implication: **the primary lever for growth is penetration - getting more people to buy you at least once** - not squeezing more out of existing fans.

## Mental Availability vs Physical Availability

Sharp identifies two assets every brand must build:

### Mental Availability
How easily does your brand come to mind in a buying situation?

This is not just awareness. It is the strength and breadth of memory structures: how many different cues (moods, occasions, contexts, needs) trigger a thought of your brand.

A brand with high mental availability gets recalled when someone is thirsty, tired, shopping on a phone, planning a party, or recovering from a run - not just when they are already in the drinks aisle.

### Physical Availability
Can buyers find and buy you easily?

Distribution, shelf placement, website UX, app store ranking, checkout flow - all of these reduce friction. A brand can have perfect mental availability and still lose the sale if it is hard to find or hard to buy.

<Callout type="tip">
Build both together. Mental availability without physical availability loses the sale at the last step. Physical availability without mental availability means buyers walk past without noticing you.
</Callout>

## Distinctive Brand Assets

Sharp argues that brands do not differentiate in the way strategy textbooks claim. Buyers do not carefully weigh attributes. They buy on habit, triggered by recognition cues.

This makes **distinctiveness** more important than **differentiation**.

Distinctive brand assets are elements buyers reliably associate with your brand:

- Colours (Cadbury purple, Tiffany blue)
- Shapes (Coca-Cola bottle silhouette)
- Characters (GEICO gecko, Michelin Man)
- Sonic logos (Intel chime, McDonald's "ba da ba ba ba")
- Taglines that survive decades of campaigns

The goal is to make these assets so consistent and so widely exposed that any one of them can trigger your brand without a logo or name present.

## What This Means for Campaign Planning

| Old Assumption | Sharp's Evidence |
|---|---|
| Target your best customers | Reach all category buyers |
| Loyalty drives growth | Penetration drives growth |
| Differentiate on attributes | Be distinctive and memorable |
| Niche = efficiency | Niche = shrinking ceiling |
| Personalize everything | Consistent assets at scale |

---

## Applying the Framework

1. **Audit your reach** - What share of category buyers saw your brand last month? If it is under 30-40%, reach is your constraint, not creative quality.
2. **Map your memory cues** - List every occasion, mood, and need state that could trigger a purchase. How many of them are you actively linking to your brand?
3. **Protect your distinctive assets** - Audit the last 12 months of creative. Are the same colours, characters, and sounds showing up consistently?
4. **Measure penetration, not just loyalty** - Add a buyer acquisition metric alongside your retention dashboard.
5. **Expand physical availability** - Remove one friction point from your purchase path this quarter.
