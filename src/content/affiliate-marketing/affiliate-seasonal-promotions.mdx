Affiliate traffic isn't flat across the year, it spikes hard around a handful of shopping moments, and the brands that plan those spikes months out consistently outperform the ones that scramble in November. In 2024, affiliate traffic drove 20.3% of Cyber Monday sales, which tells you how much of peak-season revenue now runs through partners rather than your own paid channels.

## Start the calendar earlier than feels natural

The instinct is to think about Black Friday in October, but affiliates plan their own content calendars months in advance, especially publishers building "best deals" roundup pages that need lead time to rank in search. Reach out to top affiliates 8-10 weeks before Black Friday with your planned commission structure, creative assets, and any exclusive deals you want them featuring.

Key dates to build a calendar around:

- **Amazon Prime Day**, mid-July, a major moment even for non-Amazon brands since it primes shoppers into a deal-hunting mindset
- **Black Friday / Cyber Monday**, the single biggest affiliate revenue week of the year across most verticals
- **Cyber Week extended promos**, many brands now run boosted rates from the Monday before Thanksgiving through the following Cyber Monday
- **December holiday shipping deadlines**, a distinct push separate from Black Friday, urgency here is about shipping cutoffs, not discounts
- **New Year / January "resale" period**, gift-card redemption and post-holiday sales create a smaller but real second wave

Publishing your full seasonal calendar to affiliates early lets their best-performing partners build it into their own content pipeline, instead of bolting your offer onto a page at the last minute.

## Commission boosts, done right

A limited-time boosted commission rate during Cyber Week, for example lifting a standard 10% rate to 15%, is one of the most reliable levers for getting affiliates to prioritize your offer over a competitor's in the same roundup post. The boost has to be meaningfully large to change behavior; a 1-2 point bump rarely moves anyone's editorial priorities.

<Callout type="tip">
Reward affiliates who ran your Black Friday promo with an extra bump specifically for Cyber Monday. It keeps the same publishers actively promoting through the full weekend instead of moving on to a competitor's deal once Black Friday ends.
</Callout>

Beyond a flat rate increase, tiered bonuses work well for driving volume: a structure like "$100 bonus at 20 sales, $250 at 50 sales" gives your highest-performing affiliates a reason to push harder in the final days rather than coasting once they've hit a comfortable number. Combine the flat boost (keeps everyone motivated) with a volume bonus (rewards your top 10%) rather than choosing one or the other.

## Creative refresh timing

Reusing last year's banner creative into a new season is a common shortcut, and it's usually a mistake. Pricing, product lineup, and even your brand's visual identity likely shifted, and stale creative reads as an afterthought to publishers deciding which brand's deal earns the top spot on their page.

Plan creative refresh on this rough timeline:

1. **6-8 weeks out**, finalize the actual promo offer (percentage, dollar amount, bundle) so creative can be built around real numbers
2. **4-6 weeks out**, ship new banners, email swipe copy, and updated landing pages to your affiliate network
3. **1-2 weeks out**, send a reminder push with any last details (exact start time, exclusive codes for top partners)
4. **Day-of**, monitor for broken tracking links or expired codes, the highest-traffic day of the year is the worst day to discover a tracking bug

<Callout type="warning">
Test every affiliate tracking link and promo code at least 48 hours before launch. A broken link on Black Friday morning can cost more in lost commission-tracked revenue than the entire creative refresh budget.
</Callout>

## After the peak

The week after Cyber Monday is when most brands stop paying attention, which is exactly when you should be reviewing which affiliates over- and under-performed relative to last year. Use that data to renegotiate placement priority or commission tiers for the following year's calendar, while the results are still fresh for both sides of the conversation.

Building this review into your process turns each season into a feedback loop instead of a one-off scramble. The brands that treat Black Friday as a recurring system, not an annual fire drill, are the ones compounding gains year over year.
