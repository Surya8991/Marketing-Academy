Every "best affiliate software" listicle throws a dozen tools at you and lets you sort it out. That's backwards. The right platform depends almost entirely on two things: what you sell, and how big your partner program needs to get. Once you know those, the choice narrows fast.

## Rewardful: the fastest path for Stripe-based SaaS

Rewardful plugs directly into Stripe or Paddle checkout and starts tracking recurring commissions within an afternoon. Pricing runs roughly $49-149 a month, making it the cheapest of the four by a wide margin.

It's built for one shape of business: subscription SaaS that already bills through Stripe and wants affiliates paid a percentage of recurring revenue. There's no partner marketplace, no complex approval workflows, no multi-tier partner types, just clean tracking and payouts.

Pick Rewardful when your program is genuinely simple: one commission structure, one product, and a founder or single marketer running it. Momentum matters here, you can be live before lunch.

## Tapfiliate: more control, still SMB-friendly

Tapfiliate sits a notch above Rewardful in flexibility, running about $74-179 a month on annual plans. It supports multiple commission structures at once, coupon-code tracking, custom onboarding flows, and integrations beyond just Stripe, including Shopify and WooCommerce.

That makes Tapfiliate a better fit for e-commerce brands and multi-product companies where a single flat commission rate doesn't capture reality. If you need different rates for different product lines or want coupon-based attribution alongside links, this is where you look next.

The tradeoff is setup time. You're configuring more knobs than Rewardful, which pays off once your program outgrows "one rate for everyone."

## PartnerStack: B2B partner programs at scale

PartnerStack is a different tier entirely. Beyond your own affiliate program, it gives access to a marketplace of 116,000+ B2B partners, plus tooling for referral partners, resellers, and agencies, not just content affiliates.

Expect to pay $2,000-5,000+ a month minimum, which prices out most early-stage companies. PartnerStack earns that cost when your program is a real partner-led growth motion with multiple partner types, tiered incentives, and a dedicated partnerships team managing it.

<Callout type="warning">
Don't buy PartnerStack because it's the "biggest name." If you have five affiliates and one commission rate, you're paying enterprise pricing for features you'll never touch.
</Callout>

## Impact.com: the enterprise network

Impact.com is the platform recommended most often for large, mature affiliate networks running across multiple regions, currencies, and partner types simultaneously. It handles complex attribution, fraud detection, and contract management at a scale the other three don't attempt.

The catch is the same as PartnerStack: enterprise pricing and enterprise complexity. Impact makes sense once you're running hundreds of active partners and need dedicated account management, not when you're recruiting your first ten affiliates.

Company size and program maturity, more than feature lists, determine whether Impact is overkill or exactly right.

## Choosing by company size and business model

Line these up against your actual situation rather than reading feature comparisons in isolation:

- **Early-stage SaaS on Stripe, simple commission** -> Rewardful
- **E-commerce or multi-product, need coupon tracking or tiered rates** -> Tapfiliate
- **B2B SaaS building a full partner-led growth motion** -> PartnerStack
- **Enterprise brand running a mature, multi-region affiliate network** -> Impact.com

The mistake almost every team makes is choosing based on brand recognition instead of matching the platform to their current program size, then re-platforming eighteen months later once the mismatch becomes obvious. Start with the tool that fits today's affiliate count and commission complexity, not the one you might grow into.
