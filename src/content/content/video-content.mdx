# Video Content

Video is no longer a 'nice to have' channel — it is where attention lives. By 2025, 91% of businesses use video as part of their marketing strategy, up from 86% in 2024, and 93% of marketers report strong ROI from it. This lesson is for marketers who already know they should be making video, but are stuck arguing internally about format, length, and how polished things need to look.

## What It Actually Is

Marketing video splits into two distinct disciplines that share a camera but almost nothing else. **Short-form** is anything under ~90 seconds, built vertical (9:16), optimized for the For You Page on TikTok, Reels, and Shorts. **Long-form** is 3+ minutes, usually horizontal (16:9), built for YouTube, sales pages, webinars, and lifecycle email. A single Duolingo TikTok of the green owl doing something unhinged is short-form; MrBeast's 20-minute "Survive 100 Days in a Nuclear Bunker" is long-form. Both are video. They obey opposite rules.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The numbers are lopsided enough that ignoring video is a strategic mistake:

- Videos under 90 seconds retain **50% of viewers** — roughly double the retention of long-form ([Stack Influence](https://stackinfluence.com/short-form-vs-long-form-video-drives-engagement/)).
- **66% of marketers** call short-form the most engaging social content type, and **47%** say it is more likely to go viral vs. 24% for long-form ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/social-media-video-statistics/)).
- Global short-form video ad spend hit **$111 billion in 2025** and is projected to reach **$145.8 billion by 2028** ([Vidico](https://vidico.com/news/short-form-video-statistics/)).
- Global video advertising overall exceeded **$190 billion in 2024**, with projections above $200 billion in 2025 ([Goldcast](https://www.goldcast.io/blog-post/55-video-marketing-statistics-to-drive-your-strategy-2025)).
- **63% of Gen Z** prefer short-form video over any other content format.
- Businesses using AI-driven video workflows see an **82% lift in ROI** vs. traditional production, and 41% of creators now use AI tools in production — up from 18% in 2024 ([Vidico Video Marketing Stats](https://vidico.com/news/video-marketing-statistics/)).

The split matters: short-form wins reach and engagement; long-form wins SEO, lead capture, and deep brand trust. You need both.

## How It Works / The Playbook

**1. Pick a format per objective.**
- Awareness / top-of-funnel: short-form (TikTok, Reels, Shorts).
- Consideration / education: long-form YouTube + embedded landing-page video.
- Conversion: 30–60 second product demos and customer-story cuts.

**2. Set the production bar honestly.** The bar for short-form is "iPhone, natural light, one take, hook in the first 1.5 seconds." The bar for long-form is "good audio, intentional cuts, clear narrative arc." Cinematic gear matters far less than a hook that survives the swipe.

**3. Build a hook-script-payoff structure.**
- **Hook (0–3s):** state the stakes or break a pattern.
- **Script (3–80% of runtime):** deliver the promise made by the hook.
- **Payoff / CTA (last 10%):** tell viewers exactly what to do next.

**4. Re-cut, don't re-shoot.** One 12-minute YouTube interview yields ~8 short-form clips, 3 LinkedIn posts, and a podcast episode. **55% of video marketers create content in-house**, and the repurposing math is why.

**5. Measure the right thing per format.** Short-form: average watch time, completion rate, shares. Long-form: 30-second view rate, click-through, assisted conversions.

## Common Mistakes

- **Shooting horizontal for vertical platforms.** A 16:9 ad pasted into Reels is a tell. Re-frame or re-shoot.
- **Front-loading the brand logo.** A 3-second logo intro is a 3-second hook killer. Lead with tension, end with brand.
- **Treating long-form as 'short-form, but longer.'** Long-form needs narrative structure, not just more b-roll.
- **Over-investing in gear before nailing the hook.** A $40,000 camera will not save a script that takes 8 seconds to get to the point.
- **Posting once and moving on.** The single biggest production lever in 2025 is repurposing — one shoot, ten cuts.

## Key Takeaways

- Short-form drives reach and attention; long-form drives trust and conversions. Plan for both, not one.
- The production bar is hook-first, not gear-first — vertical, well-lit, first 1.5 seconds carry the whole video.
- Repurpose ruthlessly: one long-form shoot should feed your short-form calendar for a week.
- Track watch-time and completion, not vanity views — a 5-second view is not a view.
