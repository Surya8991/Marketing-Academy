# Thought Leadership

In 2025, 95% of hidden buyers say strong thought leadership makes them more receptive to a vendor's outreach, according to the Edelman-LinkedIn B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report. That number tells you everything: the content you publish before anyone talks to sales is now the most powerful part of the funnel.

## Quick Summary

- Thought leadership is original, opinionated content that reframes how buyers think about their own problem, not how good your product is.
- The 2025 Edelman-LinkedIn report (nearly 2,000 global professionals) found 75% of executives have explored vendors they were not previously considering after reading compelling thought leadership.
- 53% of buyers say it directly influenced a purchase decision in the last year.
- Hidden buyers (finance, legal, procurement, ops) are the swing vote in most B2B deals, and they are more persuaded by thought leadership than by any other marketing format.
- Budget alone does not win: B2B firms grew thought leadership spend by 53% in 2024, yet most of the output is low quality, which creates a clear opening for anyone willing to take a real position.

## What It Actually Is

Thought leadership is original, opinionated content backed by data or hard-won experience that reframes a problem your audience already cares about. It is not a product page stretched to 2,000 words.

The simplest test: if a competitor could publish the same article with their logo swapped in, it is brand content, not thought leadership. Real thought leadership contains a point of view that someone could disagree with.

A concrete analogy: think of a surgeon who writes a column in a medical journal arguing that a widely used procedure is overused. She is not selling her services directly. But every hospital administrator who reads that column now knows who the expert is, and she will be the first call when the administrator needs a second opinion. That is the mechanism. Thought leadership builds the position before the buyer is even looking.

<Callout type="example">
Stripe's Atlas Guides are a textbook case. They do not pitch Stripe. They explain how to incorporate a Delaware C-corp, what a SAFE actually does, and how to price employee equity. The implicit POV is that starting a company should not require a lawyer for every question. Stripe earns authority because the guides are genuinely better than what a $400/hour attorney would email you. The trust created by that content is what makes someone choose Stripe when they are ready.
</Callout>

## Why It Matters (with data)

The 2025 Edelman-LinkedIn B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report surveyed nearly 2,000 global professionals across visible and hidden decision-maker roles. The survey ran March through April 2025, now in its seventh year.

Key findings:

- **75%** of executives have explored products or services they were not previously considering after reading compelling thought leadership ([Edelman 2025](https://www.edelman.com/expertise/Business-Marketing/2025-b2b-thought-leadership-report)).
- **95%** of hidden buyers say strong thought leadership makes them more receptive to sales and marketing outreach from that vendor.
- **79%** of hidden buyers say they are more likely to advocate for a vendor's proposal during the RFP process if that vendor consistently produces high-quality thought leadership.
- **53%** of buyers say thought leadership directly influenced a purchase in the last year.
- **53%** of B2B decision-makers say that when a company's thought leadership is strong, brand recognition matters less, meaning a smaller, lesser-known brand can compete with a market leader purely through content quality.
- More than **40%** of B2B deals stall because of internal misalignment within the buying group. Thought leadership that reaches hidden buyers before the deal starts is one of the few levers that changes that number.
- Fortune 100 executives estimate thought leadership ROI at $3.6 million per year, and effective programs yield **1.8x higher pipeline conversion rates** ([Column Content, 2026](https://columncontent.com/thought-leadership-statistics/)).
- Roughly **60%** of decision-makers say high-quality thought leadership makes them more willing to pay a premium to a supplier ([DSMN8](https://dsmn8.com/blog/thought-leadership-statistics-you-should-know/)).

The problem is supply-side: B2B marketers grew thought leadership budgets by 53% in 2024, yet most of the output is safe, hedged, and forgettable. The category is crowded with content that takes no real position. That is the opening for anyone willing to commit to a genuine POV.

## How It Works: The Playbook

The process has six steps. Each one is harder than it looks.

### Step 1, Pick One Defensible POV

Write the one sentence a competitor cannot copy because it is rooted in your experience or your data. "AI is changing marketing" is a topic. "AI will eliminate 60% of performance marketing roles by 2027 but double the value of strategic marketers" is a POV. A real POV is falsifiable: someone could disagree with it and make a case.

### Step 2, Anchor in Proprietary Data or Lived Experience

Edelman's research shows buyers rate "strong research and data" (55%) and "concrete guidance and case studies" (43%) as the top quality signals for thought leadership. If you cannot run a survey, mine your own product data, customer interviews, or internal benchmarks. Original data is a moat. Curated opinions are not.

### Step 3, Choose One Named Author

Hidden buyers trust people more than logos. The named author should sound like a human with real opinions, not a corporate communications department. Ghost-writing is fine; ghost-writing that sounds like a press release is not.

### Step 4, Publish on a Fixed Cadence

Consistency matters more than frequency. The 2025 Edelman study found that 90% of executives are more receptive to outreach from companies producing consistent high-quality content. Once a quarter done well beats weekly mediocrity.

### Step 5, Distribute Harder Than You Write

A single 2,000-word essay deserves a LinkedIn carousel, a short-form video clip, a sales-enablement one-pager, a podcast segment, and three follow-up email angles. Most teams publish once and stop. Distribution is where 80% of the return on your investment lives.

### Step 6, Measure the Right Things

Track inbound mentions of your POV in discovery calls. Track share of search for the phrase you are trying to own. Track weighted pipeline influenced, not impressions. If prospects are quoting your ideas back to you during sales conversations, the content is working.

## Real Company Examples

### Drift, Category Creation Through a Single POV (2015-2021)

Drift launched in 2015 with a specific, falsifiable claim: web forms were killing B2B pipeline, and real-time conversation was the replacement. Founder David Cancel and CMO Dave Gerhardt co-wrote a book, hosted a podcast called "Seeking Wisdom," and published a consistent stream of LinkedIn content arguing against the prevailing lead-capture playbook. The term "conversational marketing" went from zero to a category buyers searched by name. Drift reached a reported $1.1 billion valuation by 2021. The product was good; the POV is what made the category exist in the first place.

HubSpot has published its "State of Marketing" report annually since 2012. Each edition packages original survey data into actionable trends and benchmarks. The result: HubSpot became the default reference point for marketing benchmarks, which meant that any marketer validating a budget or strategy was looking at HubSpot data, thinking about HubSpot tools, and already in the brand's orbit before any sales conversation started. The report is free. The trust it builds is priceless.
</Callout>

### Salesforce, Ecosystem Education as Market Expansion

Salesforce built Trailhead, a free learning platform, not as a product demo but as genuine education on business and CRM skills. The implicit POV: the CRM category fails because adoption fails, and adoption fails because training is bad. Salesforce solved the training problem publicly, which made every new Salesforce-certified professional a downstream sales asset. Trailhead has issued over 9 million badges as of 2024. Each badge holder is someone who invested time in the Salesforce ecosystem before buying a license.

## Common Mistakes

- **Writing for the brand, not the reader.** If the piece could live on your "About Us" page, it is not thought leadership. Thought leadership makes the reader smarter, not your company look better.
- **Hedging every claim.** A take qualified with "it depends," "it varies," and "there are many factors" is not a take. Pick a side. A wrong but specific POV generates more useful conversation than a correct but vague one.
- **Ghostwriting that sounds like a press release.** If the named author would never say "in today's dynamic landscape" in a meeting, do not put it in their byline. Readers detect inauthenticity faster than any algorithm.
- **Publishing without a distribution plan.** Hitting publish is the beginning of the work, not the end. A piece with no distribution budget will be read by seventeen people and forgotten.
- **Measuring impressions instead of influence.** Pageviews and social likes are easy to report and nearly meaningless for thought leadership. The right question is: are prospects bringing up your ideas unprompted during sales calls? That is the only metric that tells you the content is working.
- **Confusing volume with authority.** Publishing forty mediocre posts per year buries the two pieces worth reading. Edelman found that half of B2B firms admit they are under-resourced for quality, yet keep publishing anyway. Less, sharper content compounds faster than more, softer content.

## Key Takeaways

- A real POV is one a competitor cannot republish with their logo on it. Specificity and a defensible position are what make it thought leadership.
- 95% of hidden buyers (the people in finance, legal, and ops who veto deals) are more receptive to vendors who produce consistent high-quality thought leadership.
- 75% of executives have explored vendors they were not previously considering after reading compelling thought leadership. The cold audience is the real prize.
- Proprietary data (surveys, product data, customer interviews) is the single biggest quality signal for buyers evaluating content.
- Distribution is worth more investment than the writing. One piece, many formats, sustained over weeks, beats one piece published and abandoned.
- Measure pipeline influence and buyer quotes in discovery calls, not pageviews.
