# Gated vs. Ungated Content

Every content marketer eventually faces the same fork in the road: put the form in front of the asset, or let people read it for free. Get this wrong and you either bury your best work behind a wall nobody scales, or you give away the lead-gen engine that funds the team. This lesson is for B2B and SaaS marketers who own a content program and need a defensible rule for what to gate.

## What It Actually Is

A **gated asset** sits behind a form: name, email, company, sometimes phone. The visitor pays in data to consume the content. An **ungated asset** is open — anyone can read, share, and link to it. Classic gated formats include whitepapers, benchmark reports, ROI calculators, and webinar replays. Ungated formats include blog posts, podcasts, YouTube videos, and most thought-leadership essays. The same PDF can be either — the gate is a strategic choice, not a property of the file.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The economics have shifted hard in the last two years. Ungated content gets **20 to 50 times more downloads** than the same asset behind a form, according to a 2024–2025 benchmark roundup from [Amra and Elma](https://www.amraandelma.com/gated-content-conversion-statistics/). But that does not mean kill the gates. Gated content leads convert to SQLs at a rate **31% higher than social-sourced leads**, per the same dataset.

The hybrid model has won. In 2020, **81% of B2B companies** gated at least one asset; today **76% run a hybrid strategy** with predominantly free content and a small set of selectively gated premium pieces, according to [Brixon Group's 2025 analysis](https://brixongroup.com/en/content-gating-strategies-when-b2b-content-should-be-freely-accessible-the-data-driven-guide). Overly aggressive gating now costs companies up to **57% of potential leads** in the early research phase. And only about **62% of people who fill out a form actually consume** what they downloaded — the rest abandon. Brands that switched to inline content delivery (no download, just unlock-in-place) pushed consumption to **79%**.

## How It Works / The Playbook

Use buyer stage as your gating filter. Top-of-funnel content educates a market and earns trust — gating it strangles distribution. Bottom-of-funnel content signals real buying intent — gating it qualifies leads.

1. **Ungate everything top-of-funnel.** Blog posts, definitions, how-tos, podcasts, and short videos. These earn SEO traffic and backlinks. A gate here kills the flywheel.
2. **Gate the high-effort assets.** Original research, benchmark reports, ROI calculators, templates, and webinars with named experts. The visitor is trading data for something they cannot get elsewhere.
3. **Match form length to asset value.** A 4-page checklist deserves an email field. A 60-page industry benchmark can ask for company size and role.
4. **Use progressive profiling.** First visit: email only. Return visit: job title. Third visit: team size. Do not ask for everything upfront.
5. **Track consumption, not just conversion.** If form-fills are high but content opens are below 50%, the gate is producing junk leads.

A useful rule of thumb from [ProductLed](https://productled.com/blog/when-you-should-ungate-content): if the asset is something a competitor could plausibly write next week, ungate it. If it took you two months and proprietary data to produce, gate it.

<Callout type="example">
B2B lead-gen firm Cogmis publicly moved to an **80/20 split — 80% ungated, 20% gated** — and reported record revenue months after the shift, per [Amra and Elma's 2025 roundup](https://www.amraandelma.com/gated-content-conversion-statistics/). The 80% builds inbound traffic and trust; the 20% (proprietary benchmarks and ROI tools) captures intent at the bottom of the funnel. Top-performing gated assets in their study hit **11% conversion rates** — roughly 5x the 2.35% industry average — because the gate was reserved only for assets buyers actively wanted.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes

- **Gating blog posts and SEO landing pages.** You lose backlinks, social shares, and rankings. The form earns 30 leads while the missing traffic costs 30,000 visits.
- **Asking for phone number on a first-touch ebook.** Form abandonment spikes past 70% when fields exceed five. Save the phone ask for demo-request forms.
- **Treating the gate as the finish line.** Almost 40% of people who fill the form never open the asset. Send the content inline, follow up with a sequenced email series, and measure consumption.
- **Gating thin content.** A 6-page "guide" that summarizes Wikipedia behind a form trains your audience to ignore your gates forever.
- **One-size-fits-all forms.** A returning customer should not fill out the same 8-field form a cold visitor does.

## Key Takeaways

- Default to ungated. Gate only assets buyers cannot get elsewhere — original data, calculators, or named-expert webinars.
- The hybrid 80/20 split is the current benchmark for B2B content programs.
- Form length should scale with asset value, and progressive profiling beats long forms every time.
- Measure post-gate consumption, not just form-fill volume. A lead who never reads the asset is not really a lead.
