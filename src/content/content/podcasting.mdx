# Podcasting for Marketers

Every CMO has been pitched a podcast. Most should say no. This lesson is for marketers weighing whether a branded show is worth the production cost, and what to do if the answer is yes.

## What It Actually Is

A branded podcast is an episodic audio (often video) series owned by a company, distributed through Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube, and RSS. Unlike a podcast ad, you own the show, the guest list, and the audience relationship. Shopify Masters, McKinsey Talks Talent, and a16z Podcast are textbook examples: long-form interviews that double as relationship-building tools with customers, prospects, and partners.

## Why It Matters (with data)

The numbers are real, but read them carefully. Global podcast listeners passed 584 million in 2025, with 55% of Americans tuning in monthly, and the audience is projected to top 650 million by 2027 ([Teleprompter](https://www.teleprompter.com/blog/podcast-statistics)). Worldwide podcast ad spend reached roughly $4.46 billion in 2025, up nearly 11% year over year ([Amra and Elma](https://www.amraandelma.com/best-podcast-marketing-statistics/)).

For brands considering their own show, the lift looks strong: companies with branded podcasts report 89% higher brand awareness and 57% higher brand consideration, and 75% of listeners stay attentive through entire branded episodes ([Cohost](https://www.cohostpodcasting.com/resources/podcast-statistics-marketers-should-know)). On the B2B side, the average ROI for structured shows lands around 3:1, and listeners typically complete 80% or more of an episode versus under two minutes for the average blog read ([Content Allies](https://contentallies.com/learn/roi-b2b-podcasting-metrics)).

The catch: those averages are survivorship bias. Most branded podcasts die before episode 20.

## How It Works / The Playbook

A show only earns its budget if it does a job no other channel does as well. Before you record anything:

1. **Define the job.** Pick one: pipeline (ABM-style guest funnel), brand authority (executive thought leadership), recruiting (culture and craft stories), or customer education. A show optimized for all four is optimized for none.
2. **Choose the format that matches the job.** ABM and pipeline work best with guest interviews where the guest IS the prospect. Authority work tilts toward solo or co-host narrative shows. Customer education leans toward short, evergreen explainers.
3. **Commit to 24 episodes minimum.** Algorithmic discovery on Spotify and Apple rewards consistency. Shows under 20 episodes rarely break out of single-digit download numbers per episode.
4. **Distribute the asset, do not just publish it.** A single episode should ship as the audio drop, a YouTube video cut, 4-6 short clips for LinkedIn and TikTok, a written recap, and a sales-enablement one-pager. Production cost is fixed; distribution multiplies it.
5. **Measure the right thing.** Downloads are a vanity metric for branded shows. Track guest-to-opportunity conversion, sales-team usage of clips, and assisted pipeline.

<Callout type="example">
Rebar Technology used its podcast as an account-based marketing tool, booking 48 executives from ICP-aligned companies as guests rather than chasing broad audience growth. One cybersecurity firm running the same playbook closed 70% of its largest 2024 deals from accounts that had a guest appear on the show. A separate B2B program hit 3,200 downloads per episode and 14 enterprise opportunities worth $2.1M in pipeline within six months ([Fame](https://www.fame.so/post/measuring-b2b-podcast-roi-and-pipeline)).

## Common Mistakes

- **Chasing download charts instead of pipeline.** A B2B show with 800 downloads and 12 booked guests from target accounts beats one with 8,000 downloads and zero sales conversations.
- **Hiring a host who is not the expert.** Listeners can tell within two episodes when the host is reading prep notes. Pick a credible internal voice, even if they are less polished.
- **Treating it as a content side project.** Branded shows that win have a dedicated owner, a guest pipeline, and a publishing calendar. Shows that die are someone's 15% time.
- **Skipping video.** As of 2025, YouTube is the largest podcast platform by listening hours. Audio-only shows leave most of their potential audience on the table.
- **Pitching the product on air.** The 95% of listeners who take action after hearing an ad do so because the content earned their trust first.

## Key Takeaways

- Branded podcasts work when they have one clear job: pipeline, authority, recruiting, or education. Pick one.
- The realistic benchmark is 3:1 ROI over 12-18 months, not viral downloads in month one. Commit to 24 episodes or do not start.
- Distribution and sales-team activation matter more than production polish. A scrappy show that sales actually uses beats a glossy show no one shares.
