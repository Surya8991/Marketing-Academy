# Newsletter Strategy

Algorithms change. Platforms die. Reach gets throttled the moment you ask for a click. A newsletter is the one channel where you own the relationship: a direct line into someone's inbox, with no intermediary deciding whether you get to talk today. This lesson is for marketers and operators who want to build that asset deliberately in 2025.

## What It Actually Is

A newsletter is a recurring email sent to a list of people who explicitly asked to hear from you. That is the whole product. The strategic part is choosing a tight angle (one audience, one promise, one cadence) and picking a platform that matches how you plan to monetize. Morning Brew is a newsletter. So is Lenny's Newsletter. So is the weekly note your local bakery sends about new sourdough.

## Why It Matters (with data)

Email is not a dying channel. In 2024, email users passed 4.4 billion - more than 82% of the world's internet users, according to [beehiiv's 2025 State of Email Newsletters report](https://blog.beehiiv.com/p/2025-state-of-email-newsletters-by-beehiiv). On the creator side, [Kit (formerly ConvertKit) reported](https://kit.com/resources/blog/email-marketing-stats) that its creators added 230,721,331 new subscribers in 2024, roughly 438 opt-ins per minute across the platform.

The money is real too. Substack's paying-creator count grew 65% from 2023 to 2024, and the top 27 authors on the platform now collectively earn more than $25 million a year from subscriptions, up from $7 million in 2020 according to [Fueler's Substack statistics roundup](https://fueler.io/blog/substack-usage-revenue-valuation-growth-statistics). Beehiiv saw 3,112 newsletters earn revenue through its Boosts referral marketplace in 2024 alone, per its [Most Successful Newsletters of 2024 report](https://www.beehiiv.com/blog/inside-the-most-successful-newsletters-of-2024).

## How It Works / The Playbook

**1. Pick a niche narrow enough to win.** 'Marketing' is not a niche. 'Weekly tactical breakdowns of B2B SaaS landing pages' is. The narrower the angle, the easier the subject line writes itself.

**2. Pick the right platform for your business model.**

- **Substack** - best for pure writers monetizing via paid subscriptions. Built-in discovery via Notes and the recommendation network. Takes 10% of revenue. Weakest at automation and segmentation.
- **Beehiiv** - best for operators who want to grow aggressively. Flat SaaS pricing (no revenue cut), built-in ad network, referral program, A/B testing, and Boosts (paid recommendations from other newsletters). Strongest growth toolkit.
- **Kit (ConvertKit)** - best when the newsletter is one channel inside a larger creator business with digital products, courses, or automated funnels. Strongest automation and tagging.

**3. Build the opt-in flywheel.** A landing page with a single promise, a content upgrade or lead magnet, and embedded signup forms on every blog post. Lenny Rachitsky grew Lenny's Newsletter past 900,000 subscribers largely on the back of guest posts and a referral program.

**4. Commit to a cadence and a format.** Weekly is the sweet spot for most niches. Pick a sending day, a section structure, and a length you can sustain for 52 weeks straight.

**5. Optimize three numbers, not twelve.** Open rate (subject lines and sender reputation), click rate (content quality and CTA placement), and unsubscribe rate (audience fit). Ignore vanity counts.

<Callout type="example">
Morning Brew was sold to Insider Inc. for a reported $75 million in 2020 after growing to roughly 2.5 million daily subscribers. They did it with one tactic: a referral program that gave subscribers stickers at 5 referrals, a mug at 25, and branded swag higher up. The list was the asset. The acquisition multiple was built on it.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating the newsletter as a content dumping ground instead of a single weekly promise. If readers cannot describe your newsletter in one sentence, neither can they recommend it.
- Optimizing for list size before nailing engagement. A 5,000-subscriber list with 50% opens beats a 50,000 list with 8% opens every time, both for revenue and deliverability.
- Picking Substack because it is trendy when you actually need automation. Migrating 20,000 subscribers later is painful. Choose the platform that fits where you want to be in 18 months.
- Skipping the welcome sequence. The moment someone subscribes is the highest-intent moment you will ever get - do not waste it on a bare 'thanks for subscribing' line.

## Key Takeaways

- A newsletter is the only marketing asset where you own the distribution. Build it before you need it.
- Platform choice follows business model: Substack for paid subs, Beehiiv for growth, Kit for creator businesses with products.
- Cadence and a one-sentence promise beat clever formats. Show up weekly with the same shape of value.
