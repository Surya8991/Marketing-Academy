# Webinars and Live Content

## What It Is

A webinar is a live (or pre-recorded) online presentation where a host teaches, demos, or discusses a topic with a registered audience. Attendees join via a link, can ask questions in real time, and often receive a recording afterward. Live content is the broader category - it includes webinars, live streams on LinkedIn or YouTube, virtual summits, and product demos done in front of a live audience.

B2B marketers have used webinars since the late 1990s. They keep using them because the format does something most content cannot: it holds attention for a long time. The average attendee watches a webinar for 57 minutes (Goldcast, 2024). That is 57 minutes of uninterrupted access to a potential buyer.

What makes webinars different from a blog post or a PDF is intent. A person who registers for a webinar is actively choosing to spend time with your brand. That signal of intent is what makes webinar leads so valuable for sales teams.

## Real-World Example

Drift, the conversational marketing platform, ran a webinar series called "Drift on Drift" starting in 2019. Instead of generic marketing tips, they showed exactly how their own team used their product to generate pipeline. By 2021, the series had driven over 10,000 registrations per event and became one of their top three demand generation channels. The key was specificity: they used real numbers from their own business, not hypothetical examples.

## Why It Matters

Webinars solve a specific problem in B2B sales: the education gap. Complex products and services need explanation before a prospect will agree to a sales conversation. A webinar lets you educate dozens or hundreds of prospects at once, qualify them based on engagement, and hand the warmest ones to your sales team.

According to the 2024 B2B Webinar Benchmark Report by Goldcast, 73% of B2B marketers rank webinars as their top source of high-quality leads. Webinar-driven demo bookings increased 18% year-over-year in 2024. This is not a legacy tactic - it is one of the few content formats with a direct, measurable line to pipeline.

Use webinars when:
- Your product or service needs demonstration to be understood
- Your sales cycle is longer than 30 days
- You want to build trust with a new audience segment
- You have a customer success story that is better shown than written

## How It Works

A successful webinar has five phases: planning, promotion, delivery, follow-up, and repurposing. Skipping any phase reduces the pipeline value of the event.

**Phase 1 - Planning:** Pick a topic your ideal customer is actively searching for. A narrow, specific topic ("How SaaS CFOs reduce churn in Q4") outperforms a broad one ("How to grow your SaaS business") every time.

**Phase 2 - Promotion:** Promote for at least three weeks. Use email, LinkedIn, and paid social. Expect 40-50% of registrants to attend live - that is normal. The rest will watch the recording.

**Phase 3 - Reminder sequence:** Send reminders at 1 week, 1 day, and 1 hour before the event. Each reminder should add new value - a teaser stat, a question the speaker will answer, or a sneak peek at the agenda.

**Phase 4 - Delivery:** Keep the presentation to 35-40 minutes and save 15-20 minutes for live Q and A. The Q and A is often where the best insights come out and where attendees decide whether to talk to sales.

**Phase 5 - Follow-up and repurpose:** Email attendees within 24 hours with the recording and a clear next step. Turn the recording into short clips for social media, pull quotes for blog posts, and add the transcript to your SEO content library.

<Callout type="tip">
The registration-to-attendee rate for most webinars is 40-50%. Do not judge a webinar by registrations alone. Track attendee-to-pipeline conversion rate instead. That is the number that connects webinars to revenue.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes

<Callout type="warning">
The most common mistake is treating the webinar as the finish line. Most pipeline comes from the follow-up, not the live event. If your post-event email sequence is just 'Here is the recording,' you are leaving most of the value on the table. Build a 3-5 email nurture sequence that segments attendees by engagement level and sends each group a relevant next step - a case study, a demo offer, or a free consultation.
</Callout>

A second common mistake is choosing topics that only existing customers care about. Your webinar topic should be relevant to prospects who have never heard of you. Test your topic idea by asking: 'Would someone Google this?' If not, it is probably too product-specific.

## The One-Line Takeaway

**Webinars work because they replace a sales call with a classroom - run them consistently, follow up fast, and the pipeline will follow.**
