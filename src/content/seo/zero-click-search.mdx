# Zero-Click Search

A zero-click search is when someone types a question into Google, reads the answer directly on the results page, and never clicks a single link. They find out the weather, convert currencies, or learn a definition, all without visiting any website. The session ends right there.

This used to be rare. Now it is the dominant outcome of search. According to SparkToro and Datos' July 2024 clickstream study, **58.5% of US Google searches** and **59.7% of EU searches** ended without a click to the open web. By early 2026, that number had climbed to roughly 68% of US Google searches (SparkToro/Similarweb), with mobile running even higher. For every 1,000 US searches, only about 320 clicks now reach an independent website.

## Quick Summary

- Around 60-70% of Google searches end without a click, depending on query type and device, with mobile running as high as 77%
- AI Overviews (Google's AI-generated answer summaries) now appear on roughly 48% of Google queries as of early 2026, up from around 31% in early 2025
- When an AI Overview appears, organic click-through rates drop by roughly 60%
- Rankings still matter, but SERP visibility (impressions) is now the primary metric that predicts brand value
- The fix is not to fight zero-click, it is to become the answer Google shows

## What Is a Zero-Click Search, Really?

Think of Google as a librarian. In 2010, you asked the librarian a question and she pointed you to a book (a website). You walked over and read the book yourself. That walk = a click.

Today, the librarian just tells you the answer out loud. You nod and leave. No book. No click.

Google has built a library inside its search results page. It now answers questions directly using:

- **Featured snippets** (a paragraph or list pulled from a webpage)
- **Knowledge panels** (brand or entity information on the right side of the page)
- **People Also Ask (PAA) boxes** (expandable mini-answers to related questions)
- **AI Overviews** (an AI-written summary citing multiple sources)
- **Local packs** (maps with business information for "near me" searches)
- **Direct answer boxes** (instant answers for calculations, conversions, and dates)

Each of these features intercepts the user before they reach the blue links below.

<Callout type="info">
**Zero-click is not one thing, it is a category.** A zero-click session triggered by a Knowledge Panel (Google scraping your brand info) is very different from one triggered by an AI Overview (Google summarizing your article). Your strategy for each is different. Know which feature you are up against before you optimise.
</Callout>

## Why It Matters to Your Business

### 1. Traffic is decoupling from rankings

A site can rank number one and still lose traffic. Why? Because a featured snippet or AI Overview sits above position one and absorbs the click. In one documented case tracked by Semrush, impressions more than doubled for a website between May 2024 and September 2025, while click-through rate fell from 1.5% to under 0.5%. More visible. Less traffic. Both at the same time.

### 2. AI Overviews are accelerating, not stabilising

Semrush data shows AI Overviews went from appearing on 6.49% of US desktop queries in January 2025 to 13.14% by March 2025, a 102% increase in just two months. Google has since expanded AI Overviews to reach 2 billion users across 200 countries. Every content category eventually gets an AI Overview. Informational content gets them first.

### 3. Bottom-of-funnel queries still send clicks, for now

Not all queries are equal. Transactional searches like "buy running shoes size 10" or comparison searches like "Shopify vs WooCommerce pricing" still drive strong click-through rates because the user needs to complete an action. Zero-click is mostly an informational problem. Your blog posts and how-to guides are the most exposed. Your product and pricing pages are safer, for now.

### 4. Brand impressions are the new traffic

If 75-80% of searches end on the SERP, the SERP is where your brand lives. Every time Google shows your content in a featured snippet, the user reads your brand name, even without clicking. This is free brand awareness at scale. But only if you are the source Google picks.

## Real Company Examples

<Callout type="example">
**HubSpot, featured snippets as a brand moat (2024)**

HubSpot's content team built thousands of definitional articles explicitly structured to win featured snippets. They front-loaded every article with a concise 40-60 word answer, used clear H2 question headings, and structured how-to content as numbered lists. Ahrefs' 2024 organic study showed HubSpot's blog ranking for over 1.2 million keywords and capturing featured snippets on high-intent queries like "what is a CRM" and "how to write a sales email." Even when users do not click, HubSpot's brand name appears at the top of the answer card. Their own earnings reports through 2024 showed marketing-sourced signups growing double digits, even as raw blog traffic declined. Brand visibility in zero-click results was doing the work that traffic used to do.
</Callout>

<Callout type="example">
**Investopedia, entity completeness as a citation strategy (2024-2025)**

Investopedia dominates zero-click real estate in the finance vertical by building what SEOs call "entity completeness." Every article defines related concepts, links to authoritative sources, and uses structured FAQ schema. BrightEdge's 2024 data showed Investopedia appearing in AI Overviews for over 60% of informational finance queries where it ranked in the top 10. The strategy: be so thorough about an entity (say, "compound interest") that Google trusts your page as the authoritative source to cite, not just to rank.
</Callout>

## How to Win in a Zero-Click Environment

This is not a list of hacks. It is a genuine strategic shift in how you think about SEO success.

### Step 1: Identify which queries are zero-click risks

Use Google Search Console to find keywords where your impressions are high but your CTR is under 1%. That gap, high impressions, low clicks, almost always signals a SERP feature is intercepting your traffic. Tools like Semrush Position Tracking let you see exactly which features appear for each keyword.

### Step 2: Write "snippet bait" paragraphs

For every informational page on your site, write a concise 40-60 word answer in the first paragraph, directly below the H1 or H2 heading. This paragraph should answer the question directly, without preamble. Google extracts this block into featured snippets and AI Overviews.

Bad: "In this article, we will explore the many dimensions of what compound interest means and how it works in different financial contexts..."

Good: "Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. A $1,000 deposit at 5% annual compound interest grows to $1,629 after 10 years, compared to $1,500 with simple interest."

### Step 3: Add schema markup

Schema markup (a type of structured data code you add to your HTML) tells Google exactly what your content is about. For zero-click optimisation, prioritise:

- **FAQPage schema** for question-and-answer content
- **HowTo schema** for step-by-step guides
- **Organization schema** for brand knowledge panels
- **LocalBusiness schema** for local search

You do not need to code this manually. Yoast, RankMath, and Schema Pro all generate it for you.

### Step 4: Build entity coverage

Google's AI Overviews pull from pages with high "entity completeness", meaning the page defines and connects all the key concepts within a topic. For a page about email marketing, that means covering open rates, click rates, list hygiene, deliverability, and segmentation, not just the main topic. The more entities your page covers accurately, the more likely it is to be cited in AI Overviews, even if it does not rank number one for the head term.

### Step 5: Measure what matters now

Stop reporting raw sessions as your primary SEO metric. Add these to your dashboard:

- **Impressions** (Google Search Console), your SERP presence score
- **SERP feature share**: what percentage of target keywords show your content in a feature
- **Branded search volume** (Google Trends or Semrush), zero-click is working if branded searches rise even as organic sessions fall
- **Assisted conversions**: users who saw your brand in search and later converted via a different channel

<Callout type="warning">
**The #1 mistake: hiding the answer to force a click.**

The old tactic of burying the payoff to increase time-on-page actively hurts you now. If your page does not have a clean 40-60 word answer near the top of each section, a competitor's page does, and Google will cite them in the AI Overview instead of you. You do not lose the click by giving a great answer. You lose the citation by giving a bad one.
</Callout>

## The SERP Feature Playbook by Query Type

Not every query needs the same tactic. Here is how to match your approach to the intent:

<Mermaid chart={`flowchart LR
  A[Query intent] --> B[Informational]
  A --> C[Local]
  A --> D[Transactional]
  A --> E[Navigational]
  B --> F[Snippet bait + FAQ schema + entity coverage]
  C --> G[Google Business Profile + LocalBusiness schema + reviews]
  D --> H[Product schema + pricing pages + comparison content]
  E --> I[Brand schema + knowledge panel + Wikipedia presence]`} />

**Informational queries** ("how does X work"), your biggest zero-click risk. Use snippet bait paragraphs and FAQ schema. Accept the zero-click and optimise for brand impression instead.

**Local queries** ("best X near me"), the Local Pack intercepts most clicks. Optimise your Google Business Profile obsessively. Reviews, photos, hours, and service descriptions all influence Local Pack placement.

**Transactional queries** ("buy X", "X pricing"), still drive clicks at near-historical rates. Protect these with clear pricing pages, product schema, and fast page load.

**Navigational queries** (searching for your brand name), ensure you control your Knowledge Panel. Claim your brand on Google, keep your Wikipedia (if you have one) accurate, and use Organization schema.

## Tracking Zero-Click Without Losing Your Mind

You do not need a 20-metric dashboard. You need four numbers:

1. **Impressions per keyword** (Search Console), are you visible?
2. **SERP feature presence** (Semrush or Ahrefs), are you in the features?
3. **Branded search trend** (Google Trends), is your brand name being searched more over time?
4. **Assisted conversion rate**, are zero-click impressions leading to later conversions via other channels?

If impressions are rising while sessions are falling, you are not failing. The search landscape changed. The question is whether your brand is in the room when users are forming opinions, even without clicking.

<Callout type="tip">
**Pro tip: run an entity coverage audit.**
Pull every entity Google associates with your target topic using the Google Knowledge Graph API or a tool like InLinks. Then check your pillar page against that list. Pages that cover more related entities get cited in AI Overviews more often, even when they do not rank number one for the head term. This single audit can reveal content gaps that no keyword research tool will show you.
</Callout>
