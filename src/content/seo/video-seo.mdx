Video is no longer a content format. It's a search engine. YouTube processes more searches than Bing; TikTok's search function is becoming Gen Z's Google. If your competitors are ranking videos for topics your audience is searching for, you're losing visibility on platforms where you already have production capacity.

This lesson teaches you the ranking factors, optimisation tactics, and platform-specific strategies to earn top placement on both platforms.

## Why Video Is Now a Search Engine

YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine after Google, with 2.5 billion logged-in users and 1 billion hours watched daily. People don't just browse YouTube — they search it. 'How to', 'tutorial', 'best', and 'review' queries on YouTube outnumber those same queries on Google.

TikTok search is newer but exploding. Gen Z, Gen Alpha, and increasingly Gen X use TikTok's search bar instead of Google. A 2024 study found that 40% of Gen Z chooses TikTok or Instagram search over Google for discovering products. The platform's search algorithm is still evolving, but early signs show it heavily weights watch time, engagement velocity, and captions over traditional SEO signals.

If you're a B2B company, YouTube search drives qualified leads. If you're DTC, TikTok search drives brand discovery. Both are non-negotiable.

## YouTube Ranking Factors: The Holy Trinity

YouTube's algorithm optimises for one goal: maximise watch time. Everything else flows from that metric.

**Title Keyword Match** — does your title include the exact search term? YouTube searches are keyword-heavy. A video titled 'How to Use Airtable' ranks higher for 'how to use airtable' than a video titled 'Airtable Beginner's Guide', even if the second is better content. Keyword match signals relevance immediately. Put your primary keyword in the first 3 words of the title; put your second keyword within the first 20 characters.

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)** — the percentage of searchers who click your video when they see it. YouTube shows impressions (how many search results show your video) and clicks (how many people click through). A 6% CTR on 1,000 impressions is solid; 2% suggests your title or thumbnail is weak. YouTube rewards high CTR videos with more impressions, creating a positive feedback loop.

**Watch Time %** — how much of the video people watch. A 10-minute video with a 60% average view duration (people watch 6 minutes on average) is stronger than a 5-minute video with a 50% average view duration (2.5 minutes watched). YouTube measures absolute watch time in seconds, not percentage. A 30-minute video with 40% average view duration generates more watch time than a 5-minute video with 80%.

**Engagement Velocity** — the speed of engagement in the first 24 hours. A video that gets 1,000 likes, 200 comments, and 50 shares in the first day signals quality to the algorithm, even if it has 100,000 views. This is why creators strategically release videos, pin comments asking questions, and create call-to-actions in the first 30 seconds.

These three factors compound. A strong title keyword match brings searchers. A good thumbnail and opening hook drive CTR. Strong content drives watch time. Fast engagement signals quality. Each feeds the next.

## YouTube-Specific Optimisation

**Title Structure** — lead with your keyword. Format: '[Keyword] — [benefit or curiosity gap]'. Examples: 'How to Write a Cold Email — Outreach Template That Gets Replies' or 'Airtable vs Notion — Which Database is Better in 2025'.

**Description (First 200 Characters Matter)** — YouTube shows the first 3 lines of your description before 'Show More'. Put your most important info, link, or call-to-action here. Include your keyword naturally. The description below the fold doesn't rank; it just keeps viewers engaged.

**Chapters and Timestamps** — break your video into chapters. YouTube shows these in the timeline, helps viewers jump to sections they want, and signals to the algorithm that your video is well-structured. A video with chapters gets 5-10% higher average view duration.

**End Screens and Cards** — link to your next video or playlist in the final 20 seconds. End screens are underrated. They reduce viewer drop-off when the video ends and boost your channel's average watch time per session.

**Community Tab** — if you have 1,000 subscribers, use the Community Tab to post polls, images, and text. This keeps viewers engaged between uploads and signals activity to the algorithm.

## TikTok Search Optimisation

TikTok's search algorithm is opaque, but emerging patterns show three strong signals:

**Caption Keywords** — TikTok searches captions, video text, and hashtags. A video about 'ClickUp vs Asana' needs that comparison in the caption. TikTok doesn't weight older captions less; if your video is old but the caption is tight, it can still rank.

**On-Screen Text** — text overlaid on the video is searchable. If you're showing a product walkthrough, spell out the product name and key features. This is both searchable and keeps viewers engaged (people watch videos on mute).

**Hashtags and Voice-to-Text Matching** — TikTok captions every video automatically via voice-to-text. A video where you clearly speak your keywords ranks better than one where you mumble or whisper. Hashtags carry weight (hashtag hashtags relevant to your topic), but they're secondary to caption and audio quality.

Unlike YouTube, TikTok's algorithm doesn't release watch time metrics. But engagement velocity (likes, comments, shares within the first hour) is the clearest proxy for ranking. Post your TikTok when your audience is most active, engage heavily with comments and duets in the first 60 minutes, and your video gets a boost.

## The Thumbnail-Title CTR Loop

Your thumbnail and title are the only things searchers see before clicking. They must work together.

**Thumbnail Rules** — bold, contrasting colors; large, readable text; close-up of your face or product (thumbnails are 168×94 pixels on desktop, even smaller on mobile); consistent branding (same font, color scheme, watermark). The best thumbnails create curiosity without misleading.

**Title-Thumbnail Pairing** — your title promises something; your thumbnail delivers emotion or intrigue that makes people click. 'How to Write Cold Emails' is clear but boring. 'Cold Email Template That Gets 40% Reply Rates' is specific. A thumbnail of you smiling or shocked pairs better with it than a stock photo.

Test variations. Upload three videos with the same content but different titles and thumbnails. The one with the highest CTR tells you what your audience responds to. YouTube's algorithm notices; it promotes the high-CTR version.

## Cross-Platform Video Strategy

The same video concept doesn't work everywhere. YouTube rewards long-form depth; TikTok rewards short-form novelty.

**YouTube-Optimised** — 8-15 minutes, structured chapters, intro hook, detailed explanation, strong call-to-action at the end. Each section builds on the last. Pacing is measured.

**TikTok-Optimised** — 15-60 seconds, hook in the first frame, visual variety (cuts, B-roll, text changes every 2-3 seconds), caption that reads well, trend-aware or personality-driven. Pacing is fast.

**Cross-Platform Strategy** — film the core content once (your tutorial, interview, or idea). Extract 30-second highlights for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Create a 10-minute deep-dive for YouTube. The same IP becomes three formats, three searches, three audiences.

## Video Schema Markup for Google Search

YouTube videos can appear in Google web search results as 'Video' rich results if you add VideoSchema markup to your website.

If you embed a YouTube video on your blog or website, add this schema:

```
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org/",
  "@type": "VideoObject",
  "name": "Exact video title",
  "description": "Description of the video",
  "thumbnailUrl": "https://...",
  "uploadDate": "2025-06-15",
  "duration": "PT10M30S"
}
```

Google then surfaces that video in web search results, not just YouTube search. A blog post with an embedded video + schema markup can earn both a web result and a video rich result for the same query.

## Bringing It All Together

Video search is where cold searchers become warm leads. On YouTube, a person searching 'Airtable tutorial' is ready to learn your product. On TikTok, someone searching 'no-code database for startups' is exploring an idea. Both are high-intent moments.

Title keyword match + strong CTR + deep watch time + fast engagement = top ranking. The formula is different on each platform, but the mindset is the same: searchers have intent, your job is to signal relevance immediately and hold their attention throughout.
