# AI Overviews & GEO

## What It Is

Google search results used to be a list of blue links. Now, millions of searches return an AI-generated paragraph at the very top of the page — a direct answer synthesized from multiple websites — before a single link appears. This is called an AI Overview (formerly Search Generative Experience, or SGE). Generative Engine Optimization, or GEO, is the practice of writing and structuring content so that AI search engines choose your material when generating those answers.

The shift matters because users can get an answer without clicking anything. Studies from early 2024 show AI Overviews appear on roughly 15–20% of all Google searches, with higher rates for informational and how-to queries. When your content is cited inside an AI Overview, you gain visibility at the top of the page. When it is not, you may disappear even if you rank on page one of the traditional results.

GEO is not a replacement for conventional SEO — it builds on top of it. Sites with strong E-E-A-T signals, well-structured content, and clear topical authority are consistently the ones cited in AI-generated answers. The new discipline asks you to think about your content the way a language model does: as a source it can quote, summarize, and attribute with confidence.

## Real-World Example

HubSpot restructured dozens of its pillar pages in 2023 and 2024 to include concise definitional sections, step-by-step numbered processes, and explicit attribution statements (such as citing research by name). After the rollout of AI Overviews, HubSpot's marketing blog appeared as a cited source in AI-generated answers for competitive terms like "what is a CRM" and "inbound marketing strategy." Internal data shared at INBOUND 2024 indicated that pages optimized with this structure saw a 28% increase in branded impressions even as traditional organic click-through rates declined — meaning the brand was showing up in AI answers even when users did not click through to the site.

## Why It Matters

Reach for GEO tactics whenever:

- Your site targets informational, how-to, or comparison queries where AI Overviews now dominate the top of the results page.
- You notice declining click-through rates on pages that still hold strong ranking positions — a sign that an AI answer is absorbing the clicks above you.
- You are building topical authority in a competitive niche and want your brand cited as a trusted source, not just ranked.
- You are preparing content for a category where users increasingly turn to AI-native tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) as their first stop rather than a search bar.

## How It Works

Google's AI Overviews are generated by a large language model that retrieves candidate passages from the web index, evaluates their credibility and relevance, then synthesizes a coherent answer. The model favors content that is factually specific, clearly attributed, and easy to excerpt. A 500-word meandering introduction does not help it; a two-sentence definition followed by a numbered list does.

To optimize for this, start with what practitioners call "answer-first" structure: lead every major section with a direct, quotable sentence that states the key point. Follow it with supporting evidence — statistics, examples, or named sources. Use headers that mirror the exact phrasing of common questions (not just keyword-stuffed phrases). This structure mirrors the formatting that retrieval-augmented generation systems are built to parse.

Credibility signals matter just as much as structure. The model is more likely to cite a passage when the surrounding page demonstrates expertise: named authors with credentials, links to primary research, up-to-date publication dates, and schema markup that confirms the content type. Think of each page as a potential deposition — you want every factual claim to be citable, sourced, and unambiguous.

## Common Mistakes

<Callout type="warning">
**Writing for humans only, not for retrieval.** Long narrative paragraphs that bury the key point are hard for AI systems to excerpt cleanly. If the direct answer to the query does not appear in the first two sentences of a section, the model will likely skip your passage and use a competitor's more scannable content instead.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**Treating GEO as a separate task from E-E-A-T.** Marketers sometimes try to game AI answers with surface-level tweaks — adding bullet points or FAQ schema — without addressing the underlying trust signals. AI Overviews draw heavily from the same authority metrics Google has always used. A site with thin author bios, no original research, and sparse backlinks will not get cited no matter how well-formatted its content is.
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip">
**Pro tip.** Include a short "TL;DR" or "Key Takeaway" block at the top of long articles — a two-to-three sentence synthesis of the entire piece. This is the exact passage format AI systems prefer to quote verbatim. It also makes the page more useful to human readers, so it is a zero-downside addition.
</Callout>

## The One-Line Takeaway

**GEO is not about tricking AI — it is about making your expertise so clearly structured and credibly sourced that an AI has no reason to cite anyone else.**
